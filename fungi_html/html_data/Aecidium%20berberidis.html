Notes: Cummins (1971) recognizes two subspecies of Puccinia graminis based on urediniospore size. Within subsp. griminis, Cummins (1971) recognizes two varieties, Puccinia graminis subsp. graminis var. graminis on Triticum, Aegilops, and Elymus, and Puccinia graminis subsp. graminis var. stakmanii on Avena, Hordeum, Secale, and other genera. ------ Per Abbasi et al. (2005), morphological characters proposed for subspecific classification (Urban 1967; Abbasi et al. 2002) do not represent natural groups; however, a new taxonomic concept is required.

Distribution: Widespread.

Substrate: Uredinia: leaves or most commonly sheaths, stems. Telia: sheaths, stems.

Disease Note: Heteroecious rust.

Host: Aecial host Berberis spp. Telial host Poaceae. According to Cummins (1971), Puccinia graminis subsp. graminis var. graminis occurs on Triticum, Aegilops, and Elymus, and Puccinia graminis subsp. graminis var. stakmanii occurs on Avena, Hordeum, Secale, and other genera; affects mostly cultivated cereals.
