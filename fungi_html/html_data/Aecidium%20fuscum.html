Notes: In his sanctioning work Syn. Meth. Fung. (p. 226, 1801), Persoon cited Aecidium fuscum as a synonym of Puccinia anemones. As a result, the epithet anemones has priority even though the epithet fuscum was the earliest applied to this rust. This species was listed by Hiratsuka under the name Tranzschelia fusca. According to Hiratsuka (1992) the uredinial and aecial states are unknown. Sydow (1904) listed additional synonyms under the name Puccinia fusca.

Distribution: Asia (China, USSR, Japan), Europe, North America.

Substrate: Leaves.

Disease Note: Autoecious rust.

Host: Anemone, Aquilegia, Thalictrum, and other Ranunculaceae.
