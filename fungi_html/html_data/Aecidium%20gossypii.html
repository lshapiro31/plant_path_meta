Nomenclature data for Aecidium gossypii

Puccinia cacabata Arthur & Holw. 1925 (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

= Uredo chloridis-berroi Speg. 1925

= Uredo chloridis-polydactylidis Viégas 1945

= Aecidium gossypii Ellis & Everh. 1896

= Puccinia stakmanii Presley 1943

Distribution: North America (USA - southwest, Mexico), West Indies; Central and South America.

Substrate: Leaves; also stems, carpels, peduncles, seedlings.

Disease Note: Heteroecious rust.

Host: Aecical stage: Gossypium spp; uredial and telial stages on Bouteloua and Chloris spp. (Poaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Hennen, J.F., and Cummins, G.B. 1956. Uredinales parasitizing grasses of the tribe Chlorideae. Mycologia 48: 126-162.

Mulder, J.L., and Holliday, P. 1971. Puccinia cacabata. C.M.I. Descr. Pathog. Fungi Bact. 294: 1-2.
