Nomenclature data for Aecidium hydrangeae-paniculatae

Puccinia glyceriae S. Ito 1909 (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

= Aecidium hydrangeae-paniculatae Dietel 1903

Notes: Formerly considered a taxonomic synonym of Puccinia recondita Roberge ex Desm. Oldest epithet not in correct genus.

Distribution: Asia (Japan, Korea).

Substrate: Leaves.

Disease Note: Heteroecious rust. Listed by USDA APHIS as a regulated plant pest.

Host: Glyceria spp. (Poaceae), aecial host Hydrangea spp. (Hydrangeaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Hiratsuka, N., Sato, S., Katsuya, K., Kakishima, M., Hiratsuka, Y., Kaneko, S., Ono, Y., Sato, T., Harada, Y., Hiratsuka, T., and Nakayama, K. 1992. The rust flora of Japan. Tsukuba Shuppankai, Takezono, Ibaraki, 1205 pages.
