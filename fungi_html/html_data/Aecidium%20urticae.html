= Aecidium urticae Schumach. 1803

    ≡ Dicaeoma urticae (Schumach.) Kuntze 1898  Note: Based on Aecidium urticae, with no additional description of teleomorph, therefore an anamorph in a teleomorphic genus.

= Puccinia urticata F. Kern 1917

    ≡ Puccinia caricis var. urticata (F. Kern) Arthur 1934

Notes: This name was published by Rebentisch without reference to Uredo caricis Schum. 1803, but it is sometimes erroneously cited as (Schum.) Rebent.. Kern (1917) and Cummins (1962) considered Puccinia caricis Rebent. 1804 to be distinct from Puccinia caricina DC. 1815, but Hiratsuka (1992) placed the two in synonymy and adopted the older name, Puccinia caricis Rebent. 1804. The name Puccinia caricis was applied by Winter (1887) and by Schröter (1889) to the teleomorph of Uredo urticae (=Puccinia caricina DC.), which some authors have treated as a distinct species; this explains why Puccinia caricis G. Winter 1887 and Puccinia caricis (Schumach.) Schröt. are frequently listed as illegitimate later homonyms of Puccinia caricis Rebent. 1804, and as synonyms of Puccinia caricina DC. 1815. Even if Puccinia caricina DC. 1815 is considered a distinct species, Winter (1884) cited Puccinia ciricina DC. 1815 in his protologue, therefore this would be both an illegitimate later homonym and a superfluous name. From Uredo caricina - Published as a replacement name for the illegitimate later homonym Uredo caricis Schumach. 1803 non Pers.:Pers. 1801.

Distribution: Cosmopolitan.

Substrate: Leaves.

Disease Note: Heteroecious rust.

Host: Aecial host, Urtica spp. (Urticaceae); telial host, Carex spp. (Cyperaceae). Arthur (1934) considered this rust to also occur on Ribes spp. (Grossulariaceae).
