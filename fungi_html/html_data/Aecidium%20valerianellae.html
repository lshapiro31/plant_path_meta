Nomenclature data for Aecidium valerianellae

Puccinia gladioli Castagne 1843 [1842] (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

≡Dicaeoma gladioli (Castagne) Kuntze 1898

= Uredo gladioli Duby 1830  Note: Specimen possibly from original collection contains teliospores of Puccinia gladioli, but type specimen could not be located to confirm this. Misapplied to spp. of Papulaspora and Urocystis (see Ainsworth 1949). May be treated as a nomen ambig.

= Aecidium valerianellae Biv. 1816

Notes: Oldest epithet not in the correct genus.

Distribution: Africa, Asia, Europe. Only the aecial stage has been reported from North America (USA:CA, OR, WA). Only the aecial stage has been reported from North America (USA: CA, OR, WA).

Substrate: Leaves.

Disease Note: Heteroecious rust. Listed by USDA APHIS as a regulated plant pest.

Host: Aecial state on several taxa of Valerianaceae. Telial state on Gladiolus spp. (Liliaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Ainsworth, G.C. 1949. The gladiolus smut. Trans. Brit. Mycol. Soc. 32: 255-257.

Wilson, M., and Henderson, D.M. 1966. British Rust Fungi. Cambridge Univ. Press, Cambridge, 384 pages.
