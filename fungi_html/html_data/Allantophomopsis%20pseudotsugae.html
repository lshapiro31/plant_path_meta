Notes: From Phacidiopycnis pseudotsugae - From Phomopsis strobi - Listed as a taxonomic synonym by Hahn (1957) and Smerlis (1962) but not by Nag Raj (1993). From Phacidium pulverulentum - Sanctioned by Fries 1823.

Distribution: Native to Europe and probably North America (NE and NW USA), introduced to New Zealand (Punithalingam 1976).

Substrate: Living and dead branches, stems, leaves.

Disease Note: Blue sap-stain, dieback and canker.

Host: Pinaceae, Cupressaceae.
