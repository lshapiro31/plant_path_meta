Nomenclature data for Alternaria circinans

Alternaria brassicicola (Schwein.) Wiltshire 1947 (Ascomycetes, Pleosporales)

≡ Helminthosporium brassicicola Schwein. 1832

= Helminthosporium brassicae Henn. 1902  Note: According to Willtshire (1947) the type is not extant.

= Alternaria brassicae var. microspora Brunaud 1897

= Alternaria brassicae var. minor Sacc. 1880

= Macrosporium cheiranthi var. circinans Berk. & M.A. Curtis 1875  Note: According to Wiltshire (1947) this species is a nom. nud. If this is true, this variety would be invalid (Art. 43.1).

  ≡Alternaria circinans (Berk. & M.A. Curtis) P.C. Bolle 1924

    ≡ Macrosporium commune var. circinans (Berk. & M.A. Curtis) Sacc. 1886

= Sporidesmium exitiosum f. alternarioides J.G. Kühn 1855

    ≡ Polydesmus exitiosus f. alternarioides (J.G. Kühn) J.G. Kühn 1859

= Sporidesmium exitiosum f. luxuriosum J.G. Kühn 1855

    ≡ Polydesmus exitiosus f. luxuriosum (J.G. Kühn) J.G. Kühn 1859

= Alternaria oleracea Milbrath 1922

Notes: Previous to 1947, literature reports of Alternaria brassicae may refer to this species (Wiltshire 1947).

Distribution: Cosmopolitan.

Substrate: Leaves.

Disease Note: Dark brown to almost black, circular, zonate leaf spot. Seed-borne. More common and causing more severe disease than Alternaria brassicae (Ellis 1968).

Host: Brassicaceae, especially cauliflower and white cabbage.
