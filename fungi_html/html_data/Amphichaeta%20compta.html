Nomenclature data for Amphichaeta compta

Seimatosporium rosae Corda 1833 (Ascomycetes, Xylariales)

= Pestalotia compta Sacc. 1882  Note: Corrected spelling.

  Variant spelling Pestalozzia compta Sacc. 1882  Note: Original spelling.

    ≡ Monochaetia compta (Sacc.) Sacc. & D. Sacc. 1906

  ≡Amphichaeta compta (Sacc.) Höhn. 1930

= Pestalotia compta var. ramicola Berl. & Bres. 1889

  Variant spelling Pestalozzia compta var. ramicola Berl. & Bres. 1889

    ≡ Monochaetia compta var. ramicola (Berl. & Bres.) Allesch. 1902

= Coryneum confusum Bubák & Kabát 1912

= Hendersonia cynosbati Fuckel 1869

    ≡ Cryptostictis cynosbati (Fuckel) Sacc. 1884

= Pestalotia depazeoides G.H. Otth 1868  Note: Not the illegitimate later homonym Pestalotia depazeoides Welw. & Curr. 1870 [1867] = Robillarda sessilis.

  Variant spelling Pestalozzia depazeoides G.H. Otth 1868

    ≡ Monochaetia depazeoides (G.H. Otth) Allesch. 1902

= Pestalotia intermedia Sacc., E. Bommer & M. Rousseau 1887

  Variant spelling Pestalozzia intermedia Sacc., E. Bommer & M. Rousseau 1887

= Discostroma rosae I. Brockmann 1976 [1975]

= Seiridina rubi Höhn. 1930

Notes: Brockmann (1975) listed Sporocadus caudatum and Cryptostictis ludibunda as synonyms of Seimatosporium rosae, but Nag Raj (1993) considered them synonyms of Seimatosporium salicinum.

Distribution: Europe, North America (USA: NJ, AK). Reports from Africa on Ficus are the illegitimate later homonym Pestalotia depazeoides Welwitsch & Currey (=Robillarda sessilis).

Substrate: Leaves, overwintered fruits, canes, dead and dying branches, twigs.

Disease Note: Weakly parasitic and saprobic.

Host: Rosa spp., Ribes alpinum, Rubus strigosus (Rosaceae). Reports from Africa on Ficus are the illegitimate later homonym Pestalotia depazeoides Welwitsch & Currey 1870 [1867] (=Robillarda sessilis).
