Notes: This is the type of the genus Albonectria Rossman & Samuels 1999. Calonectria flavida Massee ex Petch 1920 non (Corda) Sacc. and Calonectria squamulosa Rehm ex Weese 1924 are invalid names, nom. nud. that have been applied to this species. From Fusarium spicariae-colorantis - Originally published as Fusarium spicaria-colorantis Sacc. & Trotter ex de Jonge (in reference to the microconidial synanamorph).

Distribution: Africa, Asia, Australasia, Central America and Caribbean, North America (Mexico, USA: OK, FL), South America.

Substrate: Pods, fruits, bark of branches and trunks.

Disease Note: A wound parasite of weakened trees. Causes die-back and pod-spotting of Theobroma cacao. Also associated with cankers and die-back (Booth 1964).

Host: Woody plants of various families, including Theobroma cacao (cacao).
