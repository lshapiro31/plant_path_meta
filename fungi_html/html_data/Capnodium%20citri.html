≡Pleosphaeria citri (Briosi & Pass.) G. Arnaud 1910  Note: As nom. nov. Should be treated as comb nov.

= Meliola penzigii Sacc. 1882  Note: As 'penzigi'. Saccardo gave this new name for Penzig's homonym.

  ≡Capnodium citri Penz.  Note: Not (Pers.) Berk. & Desm. 1849. Nomen illeg.

    ≡ Phaeosaccardinula penzigii (Sacc.) W. Yamam. 1957  Note: As 'penzigi'.

    ≡ Limacinia penzigii (Sacc.) Sacc. 1899  Note: As 'penzigi'.

Notes: As '(G. Arnaud) Woron.'. The nomenclatural and taxonomic history of this fungus is much confused. The current disposition of this fungus requires additional research, and this database record should be viewed as a provisional nomenclatural effort in need of additional scrutiny. This name applies to a fungus with an ascal state.

Distribution: Widespread.

Disease Note: Sooty mold.

Host: Most reports from Citrus spp. (Rutaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Batista, C., and Ciferri, R. 1963. Capnodiales. Saccardoa 2: 1-296.

Hughes, S.J. 1976. Sooty molds. Mycologia 68: 693-820.

Polychaeton citri (Pers.) Lév. 1847 (Ascomycetes, Capnodiales)

≡ Fumago citri Pers. 1822

≡Capnodium citri (Pers.) Berk. & Desm. 1849

≡Microxyphium citri (Pers.) Speg. 1924  Note: Isonym as (Berk. and Desm.) Bat. and Cif. 1963.

≡Morfea citri (Pers.) Roze 1867

Notes: The name, Capnodium citri, has been used both as a common name as well as in a broad taxonomic sense for nearly any sooty mold on Citrus (Reynolds 1999). Several taxa are found on Citrus, and the use of this name should be abandoned (Reynolds 1999). The correct name in its specific sense is Polychaeton citri (Reynolds 1999). This name applies to an anamorph.

Distribution: Widespread.

Disease Note: Sooty mold.

Host: Most reports from Citrus spp. (Rutaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Reynolds, D.R. 1999. Capnodium citri: the sooty mold fungi comprising the taxon concept. Mycopathologia 148: 141-147.

Updated on Jan 12, 2009
