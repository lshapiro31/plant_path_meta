Nomenclature data for Ceratobasidium cereale

Ceratobasidium cereale D. Murray & L.L. Burpee 1984 (Basidiomycetes, Ceratobasidiales)

= Rhizoctonia cerealis E.P. Hoeven 1977

    ≡ Ceratorhiza cerealis (E.P. Hoeven) R.T. Moore 1987

Distribution: Temperate regions.

Substrate: Leaves, roots.

Disease Note: Sharp eyespot of wheat and other small grains, yellow patch of turfgrass and other diseases of grasses.

Host: Poaceae.

Supporting Literature:

Etheridge, J.V., Davey, L., and Christian, D.G. 2001. First report of Rhizoctonia cerealis causing sharp eyespot in Panicum virgatum in the UK. New Disease Reports 3: 17.

Murray, D.I.L., and Burpee, L.L. 1984. Ceratobasidium cereale sp. nov., the teleomorph of Rhizoctonia cerealis. Trans. Brit. Mycol. Soc. 82: 170-172.
