Nomenclature data for Ceratobasidium setariae

Ceratobasidium setariae (Sawada) Oniki, Ogoshi & T. Araki 1986 (Basidiomycetes, Ceratobasidiales)

≡ Hypochnus setariae Sawada 1912

= Sclerotium fumigatum Nakata 1930

    ≡ Rhizoctonia fumigata (Nakata) Gunnell & Webster 1987

= Ceratobasidium oryzae-sativae P.S. Gunnell & R.K. Webster 1987

= Sclerotium oryzae-sativae Sawada 1919  Note: From Rhizoctonia oryzae-sativae - Disease of rice (Mordue. C.M.I. Descr. 409. 1974). The teleomorph was described by Gunnell & Webster (Mycologia 79:737. 1987).

    ≡ Rhizoctonia oryzae-sativae (Sawada) Mordue 1974

    ≡ Ceratorhiza oryzae-sativae (Sawada) Moore 1989

Notes: Per Moore (1989), more work needs to be done to determine if Ceratobasidium setariae and C. oryzae-sativae are in fact distinct; as of 2014, this has apparently not been done.

Distribution: Worldwide in rice-growing regions.

Substrate: Leaves, sheaths, roots.

Disease Note: Aggregate sheath blight.

Host: Oryza sativa and other Poaceae; reported from Cyperus (Cyperaceae) and Zingiber (Zingiberaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Gunnell, P.S., and Webster, R.K. 1987. Ceratobasidium oryzae-sativae sp. nov., the teleomorph of Rhizoctonia oryzae-sativae and Ceratobasidium setariae comb. nov., the probable teleomorph of Rhizoctonia fumigata comb. nov. Mycologia 79: 731-736.

Moore, R.T. 1989. Ceratorhiza oryzae-sativae, a new combination for the anamorph of Cerabasidium [sic] setariae. Antonie van Leeuwenhoek Ned. Tijdschr. Hyg. 55: 393-395.

Mordue, J.E.M. 1974. Rhizoctonia oryzae-sativae. C.M.I. Descr. Pathog. Fungi Bact. 409: 1-2.
