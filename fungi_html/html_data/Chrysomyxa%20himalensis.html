Nomenclature data for Chrysomyxa himalensis

Chrysomyxa himalensis Barclay 1890 (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

    Variant spelling Chrysomyxa himalense Barclay 1890

≡Melampsoropsis himalensis (Barclay) Vatt. & D.K. Agarwal 2002  Note: As 'himalense'. As (Barclay) Arthur 1895, but no such publication could be located. Genus was published in 1906 [1905].

≡Stilbechrysomyxa himalensis (Barclay) M.M. Chen 1984  Note: As 'himalense'. The bibliographic errors in the basionym citation do not preclude valid publication.

Notes: As 'himalense'. Cummins & Hiratsuka (2003) and Crane (2005) question and/or do not accept Stilbechrysomyxa. Spaulding (1961) reported that the potential aecial host Picea smithiana could be infected through artificial inoculation, but Vattiprolu & Agarwal (2002) list the aecial and pycnial states as unknown.

Distribution: Asia.

Substrate: Foliage; leaves and leaf petioles, needles.

Disease Note: Witches broom rust. Demi-cyclic (Cummins 2002). Listed by APHIS as a regulated plant pest.

Host: Telial host: Rhododendron spp. (Ericaceae); Aecial host Picea smithiana? (Pinaceae) (see Spaulding 1961).
