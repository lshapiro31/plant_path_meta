Nomenclature data for Chrysophlyctis endobiotica

Synchytrium endobioticum (Schilb.) Percival 1909 [1910] (Chytridiomycetes, Chytridiales)

≡ Chrysophlyctis endobioticum Schilb. 1896

      Variant spelling Chrysophlyctis endobiotica Schilb.

= Synchytrium solani Massee 1910

Distribution: Europe, Asia, Africa, North America (Canada: Newfoundland, USA: MD, PA, WV), South America, New Zealand. Originated from South America, introduced to Europe in 1880's.

Substrate: Tubers, sometimes aerial shoots.

Disease Note: Potato wart disease, potato blackwart. APHIS has listed it as a Select Agent.

Host: Natural host: potato, Solanum tuberosum. Other Solanaceae including tomato, Lycopersicon esculentum, may be infected via artificial inoculation.
