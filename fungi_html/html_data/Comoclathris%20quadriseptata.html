Nomenclature data for Comoclathris quadriseptata

Comoclathris pentamera (P. Karst.) S. Ahmad 1979 (Ascomycetes, Pleosporales)

≡Clathrospora pentamera (P. Karst.) Berl. 1900

≡Platyspora pentamera (P. Karst.) Wehm. 1961

≡Pleospora pentamera P. Karst. 1872

≡Graphyllium pentamerum (P. Karst.) M.E. Barr 1990

= Pleospora aurea Ellis & Everh. 1883  Note: Year changed from 1889 (see Shoemaker 1992).

    ≡ Clathrospora aurea (Ellis) Berl. 1895

= Clathrospora passeriniana Berl. 1888

= Pleospora platyspora Sacc. 1881

    ≡ Clathrospora platyspora (Sacc.) Berl. 1888

= Clathrospora pteridis Lind 1915

= Pleospora quadriseptata Cooke & Harkn. 1885  Note: Synonymy follows Checa (2004), but see Shoemaker & Babcock 1992.

  ≡Comoclathris quadriseptata (Cooke & Harkn.) Shoemaker & C.E. Babc. 1992

= Pleospora turkestanica Rehm 1911

= Comoclathris wehmeyeri A. Pande 1980

Notes: Barr (1990) united the genera Comoclathris and Pleospora under the earlier name Graphyllium. Shoemaker & Babcock (1992) restricted the concept of Graphyllium and retained Comoclathris as a distinct genus. Here we follow Shoemaker & Babcock (1992), (but see Eriksson & Hawksworth 1993 for the counter-argument).

Distribution: North America (Canada, USA), Europe, Asia, Greenland.

Substrate: Glumes, culms, leaves, dead sheaths, stems.

Disease Note: Leaf spot.

Host: Multiple plant families, especially Poaceae (including wheat and rye) and Cyperaceae.
