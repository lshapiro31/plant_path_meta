Nomenclature data for Corticium bisporum

Athelia arachnoidea (Berk.) Jülich 1972 (Basidiomycetes, Polyporales)

≡ Corticium arachnoideum Berk. 1844

= Hypochnus bisporus J. Schröt. 1888

    ≡ Athelia bispora (J. Schröt.) Donk 1957

  ≡Corticium bisporum (J. Schröt.) Höhn. & Litsch. 1911

= Rhizoctonia carotae Rader 1948

    ≡ Fibularhizoctonia carotae (Rader) G.C. Adams & Kropp 1996

Notes: For additional synonyms, see Index Fungorum.

Distribution: Northern hemisphere.

Substrate: Dead plant parts, tubers, etc.

Disease Note: Crater rot of carrots in cold storage.

Host: Numerous hosts; noted pathogen of Daucus carota (Apiaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Adams, G.C., and Kropp, B.R. 1996. Athelia arachnoidea, the sexual state of Rhizoctonia carotae, a pathogen of carrot in cold storage. Mycologia 88: 459-472.

Mordue, J.E.M. 1974. Rhizoctonia carotae. C.M.I. Descr. Pathog. Fungi Bact. 408: 1-2.
