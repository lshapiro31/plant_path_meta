Nomenclature data for Cronartium fusiforme

Cronartium quercuum (Berk.) Miyabe ex Shirai 1899 (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

≡ Cronartium asclepiadeum var. quercuum Berk. 1874  Note: as 'quercium'

= Peridermium cerebrum Peck 1873

    ≡ Cronartium cerebrum (Peck) Hedgc. & Long 1914  Note: Valid only as an anamorph combination (ICBN, Art. 59).

= Cronartium fusiforme Hedgc. & N.R. Hunt ex Cummins 1956  Note: Nomen illeg. Not (Arthur & F. Kern) Hedgc. & N.R. Hunt 1918.

= Peridermium fusiforme Arthur & F. Kern 1906

  ≡Cronartium fusiforme (Arthur & F. Kern) Hedgc. & N.R. Hunt 1918  Note: Valid only as an anamorph combination (ICBN, Art. 59).

Notes: Burdsall and Snow (1977, Mycologia 69: 503-508) recognized 4 form species. Hedgcock and Long (1914, J. Agricultural Res.2 (3) 247-250) seemed to believe that isolates from Europe (possibly correctly named something like Cronartium quercus and its synonyms) were distinct from North American isolates. Later in 1918, Hedcock and Hunt felt that C. cerebrum and C. fusiforme were distinct species. Kaneko (2000) considers Asian isolates to be a different species, Cronatrium orientale. In light of the continuing taxonomic confusion and, in some cases, the lack of studies using modern techniques, we feel that it is best to consider the possibility that isolates from outside of the USA may be from different taxa, and thus, should be considered as potential threats.

Distribution: North Ameica, but perhaps also Asia and Europe.

Disease Note: Eastern gall rust.

Host: Pinus spp. (Pinaceae) for aecial state, Castanea spp., Quercus spp. (Fagaceae) for uredinial and telial states. From Peridermium cerebrum - Pinus spp. (Pinaceae) for aecial state, Castanea spp., Quercus spp. (Fagaceae) for uredinial and telial states.
