Nomenclature data for Cylindrocarpon magnusianum

Neonectria candida (Ehrenb.) Rossman, L. Lombard & Crous 2015 (Ascomycetes, Hypocreales)

≡ Fusarium candidum Ehrenb. 1818

≡Ramularia candida (Ehrenb.) Wollenw. 1913

≡Cylindrocarpon ehrenbergii Wollenw. 1926

= Fusarium eichleri Bres. 1903

  Variant spelling Fusarium eichlerii Bres. 1903

[= Cylindrocarpon magnusianum Wollenw. 1928 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]

= Fusarium obtusiusculum Sacc. 1881

    ≡ Cylindrocarpon obtusiusculum (Sacc.) U. Braun 1993

    ≡ Fusarium oxysporum var. obtusiusculum (Sacc.) Cif. 1924

= Neonectria ramulariae Wollenw. 1917

    ≡ Nectria ramulariae (Wollenw.) E. Müll. 1962

Notes: From Cylindrocarpon ehrenbergii - Nom. nov.; the epithet candidum was preoccupied in Cylindrocarpon.

Distribution: Widespread.

Substrate: Wood, soil, roots, tubers, fruit, seeds.

Disease Note: Canker, rots.

Host: Multiple plant families.

Spermosporina magnusiana (Sacc.) U. Braun 1993 (Ascomycetes, Incertae sedis)

≡ Septocylindrium magnusianum Sacc. 1878

≡Ramularia magnusiana (Sacc.) Lindau 1906 [1907]

≡Cylindrocarpon magnusianum (Sacc.) Wollenw. 1926  Note: Misapplied. Not Cylindrocarpon magnusianum Wollenw. 1928, described as a new species, but which is an illegitimate later homonym and a superfluous name.

Distribution: Europe, North America (Canada, USA).

Substrate: Leaves.

Disease Note: Leaf spot.

Host: Trientalis spp. (Myrsinaceae). Reports on other hosts are probably Cylindrocarpon magnusianum Wollenw. 1928

Supporting Literature:

Braun, U. 1993. New Genera of phytopathogenic Deuteromycetes. Cryptog. Bot. 4: 107-114.

Braun, U. 1995. A Monograph of Cercosporella, Ramularia and Allied Genera (Phytopathogenic Hyphomycetes), Vol. 1. IHW-Verlag, 333 pages.
