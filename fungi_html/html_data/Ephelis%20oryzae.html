Nomenclature data for Ephelis oryzae

Balansia oryzae (Syd.) Naras. & Thirum. 1943 (Ascomycetes, Hypocreales)

≡ Ephelis oryzae Syd. 1914

≡Balansia oryzae-sativae Hashioka 1971

= Ephelis pallida Pat. 1897

Notes: Balansia oryzae-sativae is a nom. nov. unnecessarily created by Hashioka. The use of the oldest epithet in the correct genus is blocked by Balansia pallida G. Winter (1892).

Distribution: LA; West Africa; China; India; New Caledonia.

Substrate: Inflorescences, seeds; a systemic infection.

Disease Note: Black choke, incense rod, false ergot, udbatta disease of rice.

Host: Multiple genera of Poaceae, most importantly Oryza sativa and Sorghum vulgare.

Supporting Literature:

Booth, C. 1979. Balansia oryzae-sativae. C.M.I. Descr. Pathog. Fungi Bact. 640: 1-2.
