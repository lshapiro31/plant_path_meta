Nomenclature data for Epicoccum hyalopes

Nigrospora sphaerica (Sacc.) E.W. Mason 1927 (Ascomycetes, Trichosphaeriales)

≡ Trichosporum sphaericum Sacc. 1882

      Variant spelling Trichosporum sphaerica Sacc. 1882

= Coniosporium extremorum Syd. & P. Syd. 1913

= Coniosporium gecevi Bubák 1912

= Epicoccum hyalopes Miyake 1910

= Epicoccum levisporum Pat. 1893

Notes: Standen (1943) considered Nigrospora sphaerica to be a synonym of Nigrospora oryzae (Berk. & Broome) Petch 1924, but Mason (1927) and Hudson (1963) consider it to be a distinct species based on conidium size (see Hudson 1963). Per Wang et al. (2017), this isuue still needs further study.

Distribution: Cosmopolitan.

Substrate: Living and dead leaves, twigs, stalks, fruits; seed-borne.

Disease Note: Saprobic or weakly parasitic on a wide variety of plants. Associated with squirter and black end disease of banana, elm wilt, damping-off in red pine, inflorescense rot of cauliflower, bark necrosis of apples, and ear and stalk rot of grasses.

Host: Multiple plant families.
