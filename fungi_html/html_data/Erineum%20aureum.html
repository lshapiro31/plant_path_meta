Nomenclature data for Erineum aureum

Taphrina rhizophora Johans. 1887 (Taphrinomycetes, Taphrinales)

≡Exoascus rhizophorus (Johanson) Sadeb. 1895

= Erineum aureum Pers. 1801

    ≡ Taphrina aurea (Pers.) Fr. 1815

    ≡ Exoascus aureus (Pers.) Sadeb. 1884

Notes: Synonomy follows Petrydesova et al. (2013); oldest epithet not in correct genus.

Distribution: Africa (Algeria), North America, Europe.

Substrate: Carpels.

Disease Note: Carpel hypertrophy.

Host: Populus alba (Salicaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Petrydesova, J., Bacigalova, K., and Sulo, P. 2013. The reassignment of three 'lost' Taphrina species (Taphrina bullata, Taphrina insititiae and Taphrina rhizophora) supported by the divergence of nuclear and mitochondrial DNA. Int. J. Syst. Evol. Microbiol. 63: 3091-3098.
