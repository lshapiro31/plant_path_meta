Nomenclature data for Erysiphe bicornis

Sawadaea bicornis (Wallr. : Fr.) Miyabe 1937 (Ascomycetes, Erysiphales)

    Variant spelling Sawadaia bicornis (Wallr. : Fr.) Miyabe 1937

≡ Alphitomorpha bicornis Wallr. 1819

≡Erysiphe bicornis (Wallr.) Link : Fr. 1824  Note: Sanctioned by Fries 1829.

≡Uncinula bicornis (Wallr. : Fr.) Lév. 1851

= Erysiphe aceris DC. 1805[1815]  Note: aceris is the older epithet, but the sanctioned epithet bicornis has priority. (Also, Sawadaea aceris = Sawadaea tulasnei).

    ≡ Uncinula aceris (DC.) Sacc. 1882

    ≡ Sawadaea aceris (DC.) Miyabe 1914  Note: Cited by Braun (1987) and others as type of Sawadaea, and listed inconstently as a synonym of S. bicornis. According to Homma (1937), this name has been applied to the fungus now considered to be S. tulasnei (Fuckel) Homma.

    ≡ Uncinula aceris var. aceris (DC.) Sacc. 1805  Note: Type variety no longer needed.

= Oidium aceris Rabenh. 1854

= Oidium dodonaeae Reichert & Volcani 1946

Notes: Often erroneously cited as (Wallr.) Homma or variants thereof. Type of Sawadaea Miyabe 1914 (as Sawadaia, corrected under Rec. 60B.1(a), but see Braun 1986).

Distribution: Cosmopolitan.

Substrate: Living leaves.

Disease Note: Powdery mildew.

Host: Primary host: maples, Acer spp. (Aceraceae). Also Aesculus (Hippocastanaceae), Alectryon and Dodonaea (Sapindaceae). Erroneous reference to occurrence on sycamore (see Mukerji 1968) probably refers to Acer pseudoplatanus, Sycamore maple.
