Nomenclature data for Eutypella aequilinearis

Eutypella aequilinearis (Schwein. : Fr.) Starbäck 1893 (Ascomycetes, Xylariales)

≡ Sphaeria aequilinearis Schwein. : Fr. 1822

≡Diatrype aequilinearis (Schwein. : Fr.) Farl. 1888

≡Valsa aequilinearis (Schwein. : Fr.) Ellis & Everh. 1892

= Diatrype capnostoma Berk. & Ravenel ex Ellis & Everh. 1892

= Diatrype nigerrima Ellis & Everh. 1904

= Sphaeria propagata Plowr. 1878

    ≡ Cryptosphaeria propagata (Plowr.) Sacc. 1882

= Cytospora vitis Mont. 1856

= Sphaeria vitis Schwein. : Fr. 1822  Note: Synonymy based on Rappaz 1987.

    ≡ Eutypella vitis (Schwein. : Fr.) Ellis & Everh.

    ≡ Valsa vitis (Schwein. : Fr.) M.A. Curtis 1873

Notes: The epithets aequilinearis and vitis were both originally published in 1822 and sanctioned by Fries in 1823 and therefore were considered by Rappaz to be equal in priority. Rappaz chose to accept aequilinearis.

Distribution: Asia (Iran), North America (USA) ; Europe.

Substrate: On twigs, stems and branches.

Disease Note: Yellowing, canker, defoliation, dieback, deformed leaves, etc.

Host: Berberis spp. (Berberidaceae), Vitis spp. (Vitaceae), Diospyros kaki (Ebenaceae).
