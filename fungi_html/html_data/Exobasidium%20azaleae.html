Exobasidium vaccinii (Fuckel) Woronin (Ustilaginomycetes, Exobasidiales)

≡ Fusidium vaccinii Fuckel 1861

= Exobasidium agauriae Henn. 1900

= Exobasidium andromedae Peck 1874

= Exobasidium angustisporum Linder 1947

= Exobasidium azaleae Peck 1874

= Exobasidium cassandrae Peck 1878

= Exobasidium discoideum Ellis 1874

= Exobasidium karstenii Sacc. & Trotter 1912

= Exobasidium ledi Karst. 1878

= Exobasidium oxycocci Rostr. ex Shear 1885

= Exobasidium parvifolii Hotson 1927

Notes: Parasitic on Ericaceae (Booth. C.M.I. Descr. 778. 1983). Red leaf disease of Vaccinium (Gardner. Pl. Dis. 69:805. 1985). From Exobasidium oxycocci - In the United States, it is known to occur on Vaccinium oxycoccos and V. macrocarpum (Shear et al. U.S.D.A. Tech. Bull. 258. 1931). On leaves.

Distribution: Widespread. From Exobasidium oxycocci - United States; Europe. From Exobasidium angustisporum - Arctic North America. From Exobasidium vaccinii - Widespread in temperate regions.

Substrate: On living leaves. From Exobasidium vaccinii - Living leaves.

Disease Note: Leaf gall, witches' broom. From Exobasidium vaccinii - Galls of leaves, shoots and buds.

Host: Genera in the Ericaceae. From Exobasidium vaccinii - Primarily on Ericaceae.
