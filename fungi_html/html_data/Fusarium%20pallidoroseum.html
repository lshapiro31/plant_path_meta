Nomenclature data for Fusarium pallidoroseum

Fusarium incarnatum (Desm.) Sacc. 1886 (Ascomycetes, Hypocreales)

≡ Fusisporium incarnatum Desm. 1838

= Fusarium diversisporum Sherb. 1915

= Pseudofusarium fusarioideum Matsush. 1971

= Fusarium pallidoroseum (Cooke) Sacc. 1886

  Variant spelling Fusarium pallido-roseum (Cooke) Sacc.

= Fusisporium pallidoroseum Cooke 1878

  Variant spelling Fusisporium pallido-roseum Cooke

= Fusarium semitectum Berk. & Ravenel 1874

= Fusarium semitectum var. majus Wollenw. 1931

= Pseudohansfordia venezuelensis (Deighton & Piroz.) de Hoog 1978

= Sympodiophora venezuelensis Deighton & Piroz. 1972

Notes: Booth & Sutton (1984) studied the type specimen of F. semitectum and decided that this was a synonym of Colletotrichum musae. However, this has not been accepted, thus F. semitectum is considered a synonym of F. incarnatum.

Distribution: Warm temperate and tropical regions.

Substrate: On leaves, roots, macro fungi and seeds.

Disease Note: Causes numerous anthracnose diseases; wilt and stem rot, stipe rot, fruit rot

Host: On diverse plant hosts; reported from Morchella importuna (Morechellaceae).
