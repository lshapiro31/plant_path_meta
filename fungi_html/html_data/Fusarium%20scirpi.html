Nomenclature data for Fusarium scirpi

Fusarium scirpi Lambotte & Fautrey 1894 (Ascomycetes, Hypocreales)

Notes: This species has often been confused with Fusarium equiseti (Leslie and Summerell 2006). Due to the nomenclatural and taxonomic confusion relating to this fungus, reports in the literature should be viewed with skepticism.

Distribution: Widespread in arid to semi-arid regions including Australia, India, Iran, South Africa, Turkey.

Disease Note: Saprophyte, fruit rots.

Host: Multiple genera in multiple families.

Supporting Literature:

Leslie, J.F., and Summerell, B.A. 2006. The Fusarium Laboratory Manual. Blackwell Publishing, Ames, Iowa, 388 pages.
