Nomenclature data for Ganoderma megaloma

Ganoderma applanatum (Pers.) Pat. 1887 (Basidiomycetes, Polyporales)

≡ Boletus applanatus Pers. 1799

≡Fomes applanatus (Pers.) Gillet 1878

≡Polyporus applanatus (Pers.) Wallr. 1833

= Polyporus megaloma Lév. 1846  Note: Based on an American type and hitherto considered to be a synonym of the above.

  ≡Ganoderma megaloma (Lév.) Bres. 1913

Notes: Moncalvo and Ryvarden(1997) note that this taxon has many synonyms, but its circumscription is in need of additional clarification. Only a few synonyms are listed here. According to Richter et al. (published online 2014), G. applanatum is a species complex in need of further investigation.

Distribution: Widespread; records from tropics are probably G. australe per Richter et al. (2014).

Substrate: Lower trunk, roots.

Disease Note: Butt rot, white heart rot, white mottled trunk; conks seldom seen on live trees, but fungus frequently established before and responsible for death.

Host: Multiple genera in multiple families.
