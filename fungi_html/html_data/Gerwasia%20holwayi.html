Nomenclature data for Gerwasia holwayi

Gerwasia imperialis (Speg.) J.C. Lindq. 1963 (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

≡ Uredo imperialis Speg. 1898

≡Mainsia imperialis (Speg.) J.C. Lindq. 1939

≡Morispora imperialis (Speg.) Salazar-Yepes, Pardo-Card. & Buriticá 2007  Note: Not available for use as the anamorph name.

= Mainsia holwayi H.S. Jacks. 1931  Note: holwayii is correct

  ≡Gerwasia holwayi (H.S. Jacks.) Buriticá 1994  Note: holwayii is correct

    ≡ Morispora holwayii (H.S. Jacks.) Salazar-Yepes, Pedro-Card. & Buriticá 2007  Note: As 'Salazar-Yepes, Pedro-Card. & Buriticá'. Valid only as a teleomorph including name and combination. Not available for use as the anamorph name.

Notes: Spegazzini described teliospores in the protologue of Uredo imperialis Speg. Thus, this has to be treated as a teleomorph name. Morispora imperialis (Speg.) Salazar-Yepes et al. can not be treated as a new anamorph combination since it was based on a teleomorph name. Morispora holwayii (H.S. Jacks.) Salazar-Yepes et al. lacks a Latin diagnosis and is not a new anamorph species (Salazar et al. 2007). The epithet, holwayi, should be corrected to holwayii.

Distribution: Central America and South America.

Disease Note: Rust.

Host: Rubus spp. (Rosaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Hernandez, J.R., and Hennen, J.F. 2003. Rust fungi causing galls, witches' brooms, and other abnormal plant growths in northwestern Argentina. Mycologia 95: 728-755.

Salazar-Yepes, M., Pardo Cardona, V.M., and Buritica, P. 2007. Species from Colombia, Ecuador and Peru belonging to the genus Gerwasia Raciborski from the order Uredinales. Caldasia 29: 105-120.
