Nomenclature data for Hypomyces purpureus

Hypomyces lactifluorum (Schwein. : Fr.) Sacc. 1860 (Ascomycetes, Hypocreales)

≡ Sphaeria lactifluorum Schwein. 1822  Note: Sanctioned by Fries, Syst. Mycol. 2:338, 1823.

= Hypomyces insignis Berk. & Curt. 1867

= Hypomyces purpureus Peck 1898  Note: Not the illegitimate later homonym Hypomyces purpureus Heinr.-Norm. 1969 (= Hypomyces rosellus).

Notes: Type species of the genus Hypomyces. The anamorph is not known.

Distribution: Central America, North America.

Substrate: Fungal sporocarps.

Disease Note: Mushrooms infected with Hypomyces lactifluorum are edible and are known under the name lobster mushroom.

Host: Lactarius spp., Russula spp., (Russulaceae), and other unidentified agarics.

Supporting Literature:
