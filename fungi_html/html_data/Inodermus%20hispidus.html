= Polyporus endocrocinus Berk. 1847

    ≡ Phaeolus endocrocinus (Berk.) Pat. 1900

= Boletus hirsutus Scop. 1772

    ≡ Inonotus hirsutus (Scop.) Murrill 1904

Notes: Boletus hirsutus Wulfen (1791), referring to a different fungus, is a sanctioned name.

Distribution: Cosmopolitan.

Substrate: Stems.

Disease Note: Heart rot. Cankers.

Host: Multiple species in multiple families, woody plants.

Supporting Literature:

Pegler, D.N., and Waterston, J.M. 1968. Inonotus hispidus. C.M.I. Descr. Pathog. Fungi Bact. 193: 1-2.
