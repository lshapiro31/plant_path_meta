Nomenclature data for Inonotus dryadeus

Pseudoinonotus dryadeus (Pers. : Fr.) T. Wagner & M. Fischer 2001 (Basidiomycetes, Hymenochaetales)

≡ Boletus dryadeus Pers. 1800

≡Inonotus dryadeus (Pers. : Fr.) Murrill 1908

≡Polyporus dryadeus (Pers. : Fr.) Fr. 1821

Distribution: Asia, Australia, Europe, North America.

Substrate: Roots, root collar; always or nearly always on very mature trees.

Disease Note: White root rot.

Host: Primarily Quercus spp. (Fagaceae), occasionally Acer spp. (Sapindaceae) and other hosts; Abies spp. and other Pinaceae in western North America.

Supporting Literature:

Krug, J.C., Setliff, E.C., and Dmytrasz, P. 2004. Inonotus dryadeus in North America: new records, hosts, distribution and incidence. Forest Pathol. 34: 285-291.

Wagner, T., and Fischer, M. 2001. Natural groups and a revised system for the European poroid Hymenochaetales (Basidiomycota) supported by nLSU rDNA sequence data. Mycol. Res. 105: 773-782.
