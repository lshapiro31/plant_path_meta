Nomenclature data for Kawakamia cyperi

Phytophthora cyperi (Ideta) Ito 1935 (Oomycetes, Pythiales)

≡ Peronospora cyperi Ideta 1903  Note: Originally published as Peronospora cyper. This name is sometimes erroneously cited as Peronospora cyperi Miyabe & Ideta based on a publication in 1904, but Ideta published the name originally in 1903 as the sole author.

≡Kawakamia cyperi (Ideta) Miyabe 1903  Note: Miyabe erroneously cited Peronospora cyperi Miyabe & Ideta, but the correct citation is Peronospora cyperi Ideta 1903 based on the first publication of the name. Sawada also published the combination Kawakamia cyperi (Ideta) Sawada, in 1942.

= Phytophthora cyperi-iriae Sawada 1931  Note: For synonymy see Waterhouse (1963) and Ho & Chang (1992).

    ≡ Kawakamia cyperi-iriae (Sawada) Sawada 1942

= Phytophthora cyperi-rotundati Sawada 1927

    ≡ Kawakamia cyperi-rotundati (Sawada) Sawada 1942

Notes: Waterhouse 1963 broadened the species concept to include Phytophthora cyperi-rotundati Sawada and P. cyperi-iriae Sawada. Ideta's original description of Peronospora cyperi in 1903 was from the host Cyperus malaccensis; Miyabe & Ideta published the name Peronospora cyperi again in 1904, this time from a specimen on Cyperus tegetiformis. Miyabe described the new genus Kawakamia with Kawakamia cyperi as the type species, citing as basionym Peronospora cyperi Miyabe & Ideta, not Peronospora cyperi Ideta. As both names refer to the same species, they should not be treated as homonyms, but instead, the author citation from Miyabe's combination should be corrected to Kawakamia cyperi (Ideta) Miyabe (Art. 33.2)

Distribution: Africa (Sudan), Asia, Europe (UK), North America (USA: TX, SC).

Substrate: Stems, leaves, peduncles, flowers.

Disease Note: Brown leaf spot and blight.

Host: Cyperus spp. (Cyperaceae). Also Digitaria ciliaris (Poaceae).
