Nomenclature data for Klebahnia gemmata

Uromyces brasiliensis Trotter 1904 (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

= Uredo dieteliana Sacc. & P. Syd. 1899

  [≡ Uredo spinulosa Dietel 1897 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]  Note: Not Uredo spinulosa (Cooke) Sacc. 1891 nor Uredo spinulosa Y. Ono 1982.

= Klebahnia gemmata Arthur 1921  Note: Published as (Berk. & Curt.) Arthur based on Uromyces gemmatus (an anamorph), but Arthur provided a description of the teleomorphic state, therefore this name is correctible under Art. 59.6.

= Uromyces gemmatus Berk. & M.A. Curtis 1868

[≡Uromyces giganteus Dietel 1897 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]  Note: Not Uromyces giganteus Speg. 1879.

= Uredo jacquemontiae Henn. 1894

= Uromyces standleyanus Arthur 1924

Notes: Uromyces brasiliensis was published as a replacement name for the illegitimate later homonym Uromyces giganteus Dietel 1897, non Speg. 1879. From Uromyces gemmatus - This name was misapplied to the teleomorphic genus Uromyces; the authors described only uredinia. Anamorph in a teleomorphic genus. From Uredo jacquemontiae - The epithet gemmata from Uromyces gemmata was the first applied to the anamorphic state of this rust, but the epithet is preoccupied in Uredo. Oldest epithet not in correct genus.

Distribution: South America, Central America, North America (Mexico), Caribbean Islands, New Guinea, Asia. From Uredo dieteliana - South America (Brazil, type).

Substrate: Leaves. From Uredo dieteliana - Leaves.

Disease Note: Autoecious rust. From Uredo dieteliana - Rust. From Uredo jacquemontiae - Autoecious rust.

Host: Jacquemontia spp. (Convolvulaceae). Also reported on Suaeda spp., Kalidium foliatum, (Chenopodiaceae) and Guadichaudia schiedeana (Malpighiaceae). From Uredo dieteliana - An undetermined species of Convolvulaceae. From Uredo jacquemontiae - Jacquemontia spp. (Convolvulaceae).
