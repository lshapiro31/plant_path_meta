    ≡ Macrophoma coronillae (Desm.) Höhn. 1910

     Camarosporium dalmaticum (Thüm.) Zachos & Tzav.-Klon. 1979

  ≡Macrophoma dalmatica (Thüm.) Berl. & Vogl. 1886

    ≡ Phoma dalmatica (Thüm.) Sacc. 1884

= Phyllosticta divergens Sacc. 1891

= Physalospora suberumpens Ellis & Everh. 1897

Notes: Slippers et al. (2004) selected an epitype, and demonstrated that Botryosphaeria ribis and Botryosphaeria parva are distinct species. Botryosphaeria berengeriana f. sp. pyricola Kogan & Sakuma is morphologically identical but causes distinct disease symptoms. Some authors consider it a taxonomic synonym, see EPPO 1993 p. 420-421. From Sphaeria coronillae - Synonymy based on Pennycook & Samuels 1985 From Phyllosticta divergens - Synonym of Fusicoccum coronillae based on van der Aa 2002.

Distribution: Cosmopolitan.

Substrate: Twigs, branches, foliage, fruit; all plant parts.

Disease Note: Canker, stem blight, dieback, fish-eye spot, fruit rot.

Host: Woody plants in multiple families.
