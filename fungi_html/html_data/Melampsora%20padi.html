= Licea strobilina Alb. & Schwein. 1805  Note: From Phelonitis strobilina - As 'Phelonites'. From Pleosporopsis strobilina - Original publication could not be located to confirm this name. Type of Pleosporopsis ?, listed as a dubious genus by Sacc. (Sylloge Fungorum III:693). Possibly confused with Pleosporopsis strobilorum Oerst. 1865 (not a synonym of Licea strobilina). Year changed from 1873. From Aecidium strobilinum - Pomatomyces strobilina is the type of the monotypic genus Pomatomyces Oerst. 1864. The genus Pomatomyces is listed as the anamorph of Thekopsora by Kirk et al. (2001), but it is not included in the list of accepted rust genera by Cummins & Hiratsuka (2003), who describe the aecial state of Thekopsora as Peridermium-type. In the absence of a published combination in Peridermium, we list the aecial state under the accepted name Aecidium strobilinum. Pomatomyces strobilina is based on Licea strobilina, which was originally classified as a myxomycete. In his sanctioning work, Fries transferred it to the myxomycetous genus Perichaena. It was subsequently misapplied to a number of ascomycetous genera by various authors. Reess (1869) recognized that the type specimen contained the aecial state of a rust fungus and made the transfer to Aecidium. It is recommended that anamorph names should not be used for this fungus.

Notes: Some additional archaic synonyms are not included here. From Licea strobilina - Originally misclassified as a myxomycete, but type contains aecial structures. From Uredo padi - The description in the exsiccata lacks reference to the teleomorph.

Distribution: Europe, Asia, Caribbean (Dominican Republic).

Substrate: Aecia on spruce cone scales, telia on leaves of Prunus spp.

Disease Note: Heteroecious rust. Listed by USDA APHIS as a regulated plant pest.

Host: Aecial host Picea spp. (Pinaceae); telial host Prunus spp. (Rosaceae).
