Nomenclature data for Microsphaera euphorbiae

Erysiphe euphorbiicola U. Braun & S. Takam. 2000 (Ascomycetes, Erysiphales)

≡Microsphaera euphorbiae Berk. & M.A. Curtis 1876  Note: The epithet euphorbiae was preoccupied in Erysiphe.

Notes: In the past, this species was thought to be based on Erysiphe euphorbiae Peck, but the two names represent distinct species (Braun 1984).

Distribution: North America, Central America, South America. Also reported from Asia (Japan).

Substrate: Living leaves.

Disease Note: Powdery mildew.

Host: Euphorbia (Euphorbiaceae), Astragalus (Fabaceae), and other plant families.

Supporting Literature:

Braun, U. 1984. Descriptions of new species and combinations in Microsphaera and Erysiphe (VI). Mycotaxon 20: 491-498.

Braun, U., and Cook, R.T.A. 2012. Taxonomy Manual of the Erysiphales (Powdery Mildews). CBS Biodivers. Ser. 11: 703.
