Nomenclature data for Monilia roreri

Moniliophthora roreri (Cif.) H.C. Evans, Stalpers, Samson & Benny 1978 (Basidiomycetes, Agaricales)

≡ Monilia roreri Cif. 1933

Notes: Originally classified as an ascomycete by Ciferri (1933). Evans et al. (1978) erected the new genus Moniliophthora with this fungus as its type species. According to Evans et al. (1978), no type material was extant; they have designated a neotype (a dried culture of CBS 324.75) (permissable under Art. 8.4). They originally considered it to be an anamorph; later Evans et al. (2002) argued that what had previously been considered conidiophores and conidia were in fact probasidia and metabasidia. Because Ciferri's protologue (1933) contained a description of what may be the teleomorphic state of this fungus, Moniliophthora roreri may be a holomorph name (Art. 59.2). The genus Moniliophthora, with M. roreri as type species, may therefore be a holomorph genus. See also Aime & Phillips-Mora (2005).

Distribution: Central America (Panama, Cuba), Caribbean (Jamaica), South America.

Substrate: Flowers, fruits.

Disease Note: Frosty pod rot, 'mancha', 'helada', watery pod rot. Incorrectly referred to as Moniliosis or Monilia disease.

Host: Chocolate, Theobroma cacao, and other Theobroma spp. (Malvaceae).
