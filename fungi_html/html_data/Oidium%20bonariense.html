Nomenclature data for Oidium bonariense

Pleochaeta polychaeta (Berk. & M.A. Curtis) Kimbr. & Korf 1963 (Ascomycetes, Erysiphales)

≡ Erysiphe polychaeta Berk. & M.A. Curtis 1876

≡Uncinula petersii Seym. 1929  Note: Replacement name.

≡Uncinulopsis polychaeta (Berk. & M.A. Curtis) Homma 1937  Note: Not (Berk. & M.A. Curtis) Wei 1941, a later homonym for the same fungus.

= Ovulariopsis bonariensis (Speg.) Speg. 1926

    ≡ Illosporium bonariense Speg. 1880

  ≡Oidium bonariense (Speg.) Speg. 1922

= Uncinula lynchii Speg. 1880

    ≡ Pleochaeta lynchii (Speg.) Speg. 1883

[≡Uncinula polychaeta (Berk. & M.A. Curtis) Massee 1889 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]  Note: Not Berk. & M.A. Curtis 1876.

Notes: Kimbrough & Korf (Mycologia 55: 619-626. 1963) provided the nomenclatural history.

Distribution: Widespread.

Substrate: Leaves.

Disease Note: Powdery mildew.

Host: Celtis spp., Trema sp. (Cannabaceae). Rare (and questionable) reports from a few other taxa.

Supporting Literature:

Braun, U., and Cook, R.T.A. 2012. Taxonomy Manual of the Erysiphales (Powdery Mildews). CBS Biodivers. Ser. 11: 703.
