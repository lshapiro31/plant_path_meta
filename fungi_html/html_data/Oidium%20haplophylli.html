Nomenclature data for Oidium haplophylli

Leveillula rutae (Jacz.) U. Braun & Crous 2012 (Ascomycetes, Erysiphales)

≡ Leveillula taurica f. rutae Jacz. 1927

≡Leveillula rutacearum f. rutae (Jacz.) Golovin 1956

= Oidium haplophylli Magnus 1900

    ≡ Oidiopsis haplophylli (Magnus) Rulamort 1986

    ≡ Ovulariopsis haplophylli (Magnus) Trav. 1913

Notes: Leveillula rutae proposed for conservation by Braun et. al (2013); accepted, 2017.

Distribution: Cosmopolitan.

Substrate: Leaves to whole plant.

Disease Note: Powdery mildew.

Host: Haplophyllum, Ruta (Rutaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Braun, U. 2013. (2210-2232) Proposals to conserve the teleomorph-typified name Blumeria against the anamorph-typified name Oidium and twenty-two teleomorph-typified powdery mildew species names against competing anamorph-typified names (Ascomycota: Erysiphaceae). Taxon 62: 1328-1331.

Braun, U., and Cook, R.T.A. 2012. Taxonomy Manual of the Erysiphales (Powdery Mildews). CBS Biodivers. Ser. 11: 703.

Fayette, J., Hendricks, K.E., Ozores-Hampton, M., Di Gioia, F., and Roberts, P.D. 2016. First report of powdery mildew caused by Oidiopsis haplophylli on garden nasturtium (Tropaeolum majus L.) in Florida. Pl. Dis. 100(3): 646-647.
