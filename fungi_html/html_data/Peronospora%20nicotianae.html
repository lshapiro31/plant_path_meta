Nomenclature data for Peronospora nicotianae

Peronospora hyoscyami (Rabenh.) de Bary 1863 (Oomycetes, Peronosporales)

≡ Peronospora effusa var. hyoscyami Rabenh. 1860

= Peronospora dubia Berl. 1898

= Peronospora nicotianae Speg. 1891  Note: Nomen confusum; mixed elements.

= Peronospora tabacina D.B. Adam 1933

= Peronospora tabacina var. solani Zeng (?) 1990  Note: This name was reported by Yu (1998) as published by Zeng in J. Yunnan Agr. Univ. 5(3):157 in 1990. The protologue was not available for examination to confirm that this name was validly published. Possible synonymy with Peronospora hyoscyami requires confirmation.

Notes: Several formae speciales have been described, including Peronospora hyoscyami f. sp. tabacina, which is morphologically indistinguishable from Peronospora hyoscyami, but displays differences in pathogenicity and host range (Hall 1989). The blue mould of tobacco is commonly reported under this name (e.g., Hall 1989), or under the synonymous name Peronospora tabacina (e.g. Voglmayr 2003). Use of f. sp. for a taxon infecting multiple genera of host plants is generally considered to be inappropriate, and we instead recommend use of the name Peronospora hyoscyami for the blue mould of tobacco.

Distribution: Cosmopolitan (Hall 1989).

Substrate: Leaves, stems.

Disease Note: Downy mildew. Blue mould of tobacco, a severe and economically significant disease.

Host: Solanaceae. Several f. sp. are distinguished by host range: Peronospora hyoscyami f. sp. hyoscyami is pathogenic for Hyoscyamus spp.. Peronospora hyoscyami f. sp. tabacina is pathogenic for Nicotiana spp. including Nicotiana tabacum (tobacco), Capsicum annuum, Solanum spp. and Lycopersicon esculentum,
