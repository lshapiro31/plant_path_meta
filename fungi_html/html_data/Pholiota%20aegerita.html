Nomenclature data for Pholiota aegerita

Agrocybe aegerita (V. Brig.) Singer 1951 [1949] (Basidiomycetes, Agaricales)

    Variant spelling Agrocybe aegirita (V. Brig.) Singer 1951 [1949]

≡ Agaricus aegerita V. Brig. 1837

      Variant spelling Agaricus aegirita V. Brig. 1837  Note: Original spelling.

≡Pholiota aegerita (V. Brig.) Quél. 1872

Notes: Singer lists Pholiota cylindracea Gillet; ?Agaricus cylindricus D.C. ex Fr.; Pholiota crassivela (Speg.) Sacc.; Pholiota impudica Speg.; Pholiota phylicigena (Berk.) Sacc.; and Agaricus poppiarello Viv. as synonyms but with the comment that this is probably a collective species with considerable morphological variability. The epithet was corrected to aegerita by Saccardo (Syll. Fung. V: 743, 1887) (etymology, from Greek; transliterated incorrectly by Briganti).

Distribution: North America (USA: ID), Europe, Asia.

Substrate: Wood.

Disease Note: A commercially cultivated edible mushroom.

Host: Multiple woody plant families.
