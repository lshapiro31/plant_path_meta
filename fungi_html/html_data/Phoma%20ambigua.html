Nomenclature data for Phoma ambigua

Diaporthe ambigua Nitschke 1870 (Ascomycetes, Diaporthales)

= Phoma ambigua Sacc. 1879

    ≡ Phomopsis ambigua (Sacc.) Traverso 1906

Notes: Saccardo introduced the anamorph name in 1879 in Michelia 1. This is not to be confused with a later homonym that Saccardo described from Medicago in 1880 in Michelia 2.

Distribution: Widespread.

Substrate: Trunk, rootstock.

Disease Note: Twig blight; canker.

Host: Various hosts; important pathogen of Malus spp. and Pyrus spp. (Rosaceae)
