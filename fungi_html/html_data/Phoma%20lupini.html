Nomenclature data for Phoma lupini

Plenodomus lupini (Ellis & Everh.) Gruyter, Aveskamp & Verkley 2012 (Ascomycetes, Pleosporales)

≡ Phoma lupini Ellis & Everh. 1884

≡Asteromella lupini (Ellis & Everh.) Petr. 1955

≡Sphaeropsis lupini (Ellis & Everh.) Kuntze 1898

= Phyllosticta ferax Ellis & Everh. 1894

= Phaeosphaeria lupini Syd. & P. Syd. 1904

= Phyllosticta lupini Bonar 1928

[≡Stictochorella lupini (Ellis & Everh.) Syd. 1924 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]

= Stictochorella lupini Syd. 1922

Notes: From Stictochorella lupini - Not Syd. 1922. From Stictochorella lupini - Not (Ellis & Everh.) Syd. 1924. From Phoma lupini - Not Cooke & Harkn. 1885 nor N.F. Buchw. 1958.

Distribution: North America, South America.

Substrate: Leaves, stems, pods, seed-borne.

Disease Note: Leaf, stem, and pod spot.

Host: Lupinus spp. (Fabaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Leptosphaeria pedicularis (Fuckel) Gruyter, Aveskamp & Verkley 2012 (Ascomycetes, Pleosporales)

≡ Phoma pedicularis Fuckel 1874

      Variant spelling Phoma pedicularidis Fuckel 1874

= Phoma barbari Cooke 1885

= Sphaeronaema gentianae Moesz 1915

    ≡ Plenodomus gentianae (Moesz) Petr. 1925

= Plenodomus helveticus Petr. 1948

= Plenodomus karii Petr. 1936

= Phoma lingam f. linariae Sacc. & Paoli 1889

[= Ascochyta pedicularis (Fuckel) Arx 1963 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]

= Phoma prominens Bres. 1926

    ≡ Plenodomus prominens (Bres.) Petr. ex Arx 1950

= Plenodomus sphaerosporus Petr. 1942

= Plenodomus svalbardensis Lind 1928

Notes: From Ascochyta pedicularis - Not (Rostr.) Arx 1957. As 'pedicularidis'. Misapplied (Boerema 2004). From Phoma pedicularis f. caulicola - Invalid name, without description. From Phoma pedicularis - As pedicularidis. Not Wehm 1946. From Sphaeronaema gentianae - As 'Sphaeronema'. From Phoma lupini - Not Ellis & Everh. 1884 nor Cooke & Harkn. 1885. Synonymy with P. pedicularis Fuckel uncertain. From Phoma acuta f. gentianae - Invalid name, without description.

Distribution: From Phoma pedicularis - Europe, Asia (cold regions). Reports in America are Phoma herbicola Wehm. 1946 [=Phoma pedicularis Wehm. 1946 illeg. later hom.]

Substrate: From Phoma pedicularis - Dead stems, leaves, seeds.

Host: From Phoma pedicularis - Herbaceous plants, often Pedicularis, Veronica (Scrophulareaceae), and Gentiana (Gentianaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Boerema, G.H., De Gruyter, J., Noordeloos, M.E., and Hamers, M.E.C. 2004. Phoma identification manual: differentiation of specific and infra-specific taxa in culture. CABI Publishing, 470 pages.

De Gruyter, J., Woudenberg, J.H.C., Aveskamp, M.M., Verkley, G.J.M., Groenewald, J.Z., and Crous, P.W. 2013. Redisposition of phoma-like anamorphs in Pleosporales. Stud. Mycol. 75: 1-36.
