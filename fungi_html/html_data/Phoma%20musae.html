Nomenclature data for Phoma musae

Phyllosticta musarum (Cooke) Aa 1973 (Ascomycetes, Dothideales)

≡ Sphaeropsis musarum Cooke 1880

≡Macrophoma musae (Sacc.) Berl. & Voglino 1886

≡Phoma musae Sacc. 1884

≡Phyllostictina musarum (Cooke) Petr. 1931

[= Phoma musae C.W. Carp. 1919 [1918] - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]

Notes: The epithet musarum was preoccupied in Phoma so Saccardo introduced the replacement name Phoma musae Sacc. 1884.

Distribution: Widespread.

Substrate: Leaves, fruits.

Disease Note: Leaf spot, fruit spot. Banana freckle disease; banana black spot disease.

Host: Musa spp. (Musaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Van Der AA, H.A. 1973. Studies in Phyllosticta I. Stud. Mycol. 5: 1-110.

Van Der AA, H.A., and Vanev, S. 2002. A revision of the species described in Phyllosticta. Centraalbureau voor Schimmelcultures, Utrecht, 510 pages.

Didymella musae (P. Joly) Qian Chen & L. Cai 2015 (Ascomycetes, Pleosporales)

≡ Peyronellaea musae P. Joly 1961

≡Phoma jolyana Piroz. & Morgan-Jones 1968

≡Phoma jolyana var. jolyana Piroz. & Morgan-Jones 1968

≡Phoma jolyi M. Morelet 1968

[≡Phoma musae (P. Joly) Boerema, Dorenb. & Kesteren 1965 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]

= Peyronellaea nainiensis Tandon & Bilgrami 1961

Notes: From Phoma musae - Not (Cooke) Sacc. 1884 nor C.W. Carp. 1919[1918] From Peyronellaea nainiensis - Boerema has 'nainensis'. From Phoma jolyana - Nom. nov. From Phoma jolyi - Nom. nov.

Distribution: Widespread based on scattered reports.

Substrate: Living leaves.

Disease Note: Leaf spot. Probably a secondary invader of diseased plant tissue.

Host: Diverse hosts.
