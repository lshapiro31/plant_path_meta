Nomenclature data for Phoma sanguinolenta

Plenodomus libanotidis (Fuckel) Gruyter, Aveskamp & Verkley 2012 (Ascomycetes, Pleosporales)

≡ Pleospora libanotidis Fuckel 1873

≡Leptosphaeria libanotidis (Fuckel) Sacc. 1883

  Variant spelling Leptosphaeria libanotidis (Fuckel) Niessl 1876

= Phoma sanguinolenta Rostr. 1888

    ≡ Phoma rostrupii Sacc. 1895  Note: Leptosphaeria libanotidis (Fuckel) Sacc. 1883 (as "libanotis") sometimes given as L. libanotidis (Fuckel) Niessl 1876, but the latter combination is not mentioned in Niessl. Phoma rostrupii is a nom. nov.

Notes: From Phoma sanguinolenta - Not Grove 1885.

Distribution: Europe.

Substrate: Roots, stems, inflorescences.

Disease Note: Phoma root rot, seedling dieback, carrot phomosis.

Host: Various Apiaceae.

Supporting Literature:

De Gruyter, J., Woudenberg, J.H.C., Aveskamp, M.M., Verkley, G.J.M., Groenewald, J.Z., and Crous, P.W. 2013. Redisposition of phoma-like anamorphs in Pleosporales. Stud. Mycol. 75: 1-36.

Updated on Dec 18, 2013

Leptosphaeria purpurea Rehm 1882 (Ascomycetes, Pleosporales)

= Phoma sanguinolenta Grove 1885

    ≡ Phoma rubella Grove non Cooke 1885  Note: June 1885 not Cooke Sept 1885

    ≡ Phoma grovei Berl. & Voglino 1892  Note: Superfluous new name (illegitimate).

Notes: From Phoma sanguinolenta - Not Rostr. 1888.

Distribution: North America (Canada), Europe. Report in Asia (China) not listed by Boerema (2004).

Substrate: Dead stems.

Host: Cirsium spp. and other Asteraceae, sometimes on other host families.

Supporting Literature:

Boerema, G.H., De Gruyter, J., Noordeloos, M.E., and Hamers, M.E.C. 2004. Phoma identification manual: differentiation of specific and infra-specific taxa in culture. CABI Publishing, 470 pages.

Boerema, G.H., De Gruyter, J., and Van Kesteren, H.A. 1994. Contributions towards a monograph of Phoma (Coelomycetes) - III. 1.Section Plenodomus: taxa often with a Leptosphaeria teleomorph. Persoonia 15: 431-487.
