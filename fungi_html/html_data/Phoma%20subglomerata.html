Nomenclature data for Phoma subglomerata

Didymella subglomerata (Boerema, Gruyter & Noordel.) Qian Chen & L. Cai 2015 (Ascomycetes, Pleosporales)

≡ Phoma subglomerata Boerema, Gruyter & Noordel. 1993

≡Peyronellaea subglomerata (Boerema, Gruyter & Noordel.) Aveskamp, Gruyter & Verkley 2010

≡Ascochyta trachelospermi Fabric. 1951

Notes: Phoma subglomerata is a nom. nov. created to prevent conflict with Phoma trachelospermi Tassi (1900) since 'trachelospermi' is occupied in Phoma. Didymella subglomerata is nomenclaturally superfluous and therefore illegitimate (Art. 52.1), as is Peyronellaea subglomerata; the oldest epithet is provided by Aschochyta trachelospermi, which is not occupied in Didymella or Peyronellaea and should have been adopted in both cases.

Distribution: Africa (South Africa), Europe, (Italy, Poland), North America (Mexico, USA).

Substrate: Plurivorous.

Disease Note: Leaf spot: opportunistic parasite.

Host: Poaceae, including Triticum aestivum (wheat) and Zea mays (corn).
