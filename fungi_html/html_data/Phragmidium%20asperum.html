Nomenclature data for Phragmidium asperum

Phragmidium violaceum (Schultz) G. Winter 1880 (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

≡ Puccinia violacea Schultz 1806

= Phragmidium asperum Wallr. 1833

Distribution: Europe, Africa. Introduced to New Zealand, Chile, Australia (?) as a biocontrol agent. Recorded once in North America (USA: MS, see Laundon 1969).

Substrate: Leaves, stems.

Disease Note: Rust.

Host: Rubus spp. (Rosaceae).

Supporting Literature:
