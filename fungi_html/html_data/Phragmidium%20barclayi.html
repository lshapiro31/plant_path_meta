Nomenclature data for Phragmidium barclayi

Phragmidium barclayi Dietel 1890 (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

Notes: Barclay did not descibe Phragmidium rubi as a new taxon. He believed a fungus from Asia was the same as P. rubi, a previously described fungus from Europe that is now known as Phragmidium bulbosum. Historical literature has incorrectly attributed a new species name to Barclay. Dietel described Phragmidium barclayi from material that was Phragmidium rubi sensu Barclay.

Distribution: Asia. Reports using P. rubi uncertain.

Substrate: Leaves.

Disease Note: Rust.

Host: Rubus spp. (Rosaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Dietel, P. 1890. Uredineen aus dem Himalaya. Hedwigia 29: 259-270.
