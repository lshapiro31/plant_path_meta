Nomenclature data for Phyllosticta anacardiacearum

Phyllosticta capitalensis Henn. 1908 (Ascomycetes, Dothideales)

= Guignardia endophyllicola Okane, Nakagiri & Tad. Ito 2001

= Guignardia mangiferae A.J. Roy 1968 [1967]

= Phyllostictina mangiferae Bat. & A.F. Vital 1952  Note: See van der Aa 1973 for synonymy.

  ≡Phyllosticta anacardiacearum Aa 1973  Note: Nom. nov. See Baayen et al. 2002 for synonymy.

= Phyllosticta ophiopogonis Wulandari, Wikee & Hyde 2012

= Phyllosticta phalenopsidis Marchal & Verpl. 1927 [1926]  Note: See van der Aa 1973 for synonymy.

  Variant spelling Phyllosticta phalaenopsidis Marchal & Verpl. 1927 [1936]

= Phyllosticta phalenopsidis var. vanillae Verpl. & Claess. 1934  Note: See van der Aa 1973 for synonymy.

= Guignardia psidii Ullasa & Rawal 1984

= Phyllostictina pyriformis E.K. Cash & A.M.J. Watson 1955  Note: See van der Aa 1973 for synonymy.

= Phyllostictina theicola Petr. 1927  Note: The author citation was corrected from (Siemaszko) Petr. because Macrophoma theicola Siemaszko was an illegitimate later homonym, Art. 58.1.

    ≡ Phyllosticta theacearum Aa 1973

  [≡ Macrophoma theicola Siemaszko 1923 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]  Note: Illegitimate later homonym of Macrophoma theicola Petch 1917.

Notes: Phyllosticta anacardiacearum is a nom. nov. created by Aa to prevent conflict with Phyllosticta mangiferae Bat. & A.F. Vital (1952). Phyllosticta theacearum is a nom. nov. created to prevent conflict with Phyllosticta theicola Curzi (1926). Previously reported as the non-pathogenic strain of Guignardia citricarpa, causative agent of Citrus black spot, but now considered to be a distinct species (Baayen et al. 2002). Many reports of Guignardia citricarpa in the historical literature actually refer to Guignardia mangiferae. Furthermore, Glienke et al. (2011) treated the anamorph Phyllosticta capitalensis as a distinct species. Several synonyms listed by previous authors for G. mangiferae and P. capitalensis have not been addressed by the recent authors.

Distribution: Widespread, but uncertain due to taxonomic revision.

Substrate: Leaves, fruits, pseudobulbs.

Disease Note: Leaf spot, lesions; frequently isolated as a non-pathogenic endophyte.

Host: Several hosts is numerous families, but uncertain due to taxonomic revision.
