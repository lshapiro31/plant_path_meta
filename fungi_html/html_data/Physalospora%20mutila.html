Nomenclature data for Physalospora mutila

Diplodia mutila (Fr. : Fr.) Mont. 1834 (Ascomycetes, Dothideales)

≡ Sphaeria mutila Fr. : Fr. 1823

≡Physalospora mutila (Fr. : Fr.) N. Stevens 1936  Note: As comb. nov. and intended as a teleomorph name, but it is invalid as a new name for the teleomorph since no Latin diagnosis was supplied. This name is valid as a new combinaton based on an anamorphic type in accordance with ICBN Art. 59.

= Diplodia pittospori Cooke & Harkn. 1884

= Botryosphaeria stevensii Shoemaker 1964

Notes: Stevens (1933) lists more synonyms not shown here.

Distribution: Europe (England, France, Italy, Portugal), Africa (South Africa), North America (USA: CA). Numerous other reports from elsewhere, many of which may be misidentifications.

Substrate: Bark, rachis.

Disease Note: Cankers, dieback.

Host: Confirmed hosts per Phillips et al. (2013): Chamaecyparis lawsoniana (Cupressaceae), Fraxinis (Oleaceae), Malus (Rosaceae), Populus (Salicaceae), Taxus baccata (Taxaceae), Vitis vinifera (Vitaceae). Per Mohammadi (2014), Phoenix dactylifera (Arecaceae). Numerous other host reports, many of which may be misidentifications.
