Nomenclature data for Physalospora tucumanensis

Colletotrichum falcatum Went 1893 (Ascomycetes, Phyllachorales)

= Physalospora tucumanensis Speg. 1896

    ≡ Glomerella tucumanensis (Speg.) Arx & E. Müll. 1954

Notes: Prihastuti et al. (2010) overlooked original material (illustrations in the protologue) that should been considered for lectotypification when they designated a neotype.

Distribution: Widespread in subtropical to tropical regions.

Substrate: Leaves.

Disease Note: Red rot of sugarcane.

Host: Several genera and species of Poaceae especially Saccharum officinarum.

Supporting Literature:

Prihastuti, H., Cai, L., Crouch, J.A., Phoulivong, S., Moslem, M.A., McKenzie, E.H.C., and Hyde, K.D. 2010. Neotypification of Colletotrichum falcatum, the causative agent of red-rot disease in sugarcane. Sydowia 62: 283-293.
