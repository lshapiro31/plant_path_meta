Nomenclature data for Polyporus sulphureus

Laetiporus sulphureus (Bull. : Fr.) Murrill 1920 (Basidiomycetes, Polyporales)

≡ Boletus sulphureus Bull. 1789

≡Polyporus sulphureus (Bull. : Fr.) Fr. 1821

Notes: For numerous additional synonyms, see Index Fungorum. Several now-separated species formerly classified under this taxon; some synonyms not easily attributable to a particular species. Host and range records should be treated with caution, especially, in this case, with reports from outside eastern North America. Conspecificity with European populations uncertain.

Distribution: North America (Canada, USA; eastern).

Substrate: Trunks, logs.

Disease Note: Heart-rot; also saprobic.

Host: Hardwoods, especially Quercus spp. (Fagaceae); records from eastern USA on Pinaceae are probably L. huroniensis - see Burdsall & Banik (2001).

Supporting Literature:

Rajchenberg, M., and Robledo, G. 2013. Pathogenic polypores in Argentina. Forest Pathol. 43: 171-184.
