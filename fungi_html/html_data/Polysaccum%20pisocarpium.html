Nomenclature data for Polysaccum pisocarpium

Pisolithus arhizus (Scop.) Rauschert 1959 (Basidiomycetes, Boletales)

≡ Lycoperdon arrizon Scop. 1786

= Polysaccum pisocarpium Fr. 1829

= Scleroderma tinctorium Pers. 1801  Note: From Pisolithus tinctorius - Mycorrhizal.

    ≡ Pisolithus tinctorius (Pers.) Coker & Couch 1928

Notes: For numerous additional synonyms, see Index Fungorum. The specific epithet tinctorius, although younger, is far more commonly used. Per Hosaka (2009), this genus is in need of further study and the taxonomic situation may be then clarified.

Distribution: Widespread; taxonomic uncertainty makes true distribution difficult to discern.

Substrate: Terrestrial/subterranian.

Disease Note: A beneficial mycorrhizal fungus; not a pathogen.

Host: Numerous hosts.

Supporting Literature:

Hosaka, K. 2009. Phylogeography of the genus Pisolithus revisited with some additional taxa from New Caledonia and Japan. Bull. Natl. Mus. Nat. Sci., Ser. B 35: 151-167.
