Nomenclature data for Rhizoctonia violacea

Helicobasidium purpureum (Tul.) Pat. 1885 (Ustilaginomycetes, Incertae sedis)

= Protonema brebissonii Desm. 1834

    ≡ Helicobasidium brebissonii (Desm.) Donk 1958

= Sclerotium crocorum Pers. : Fr. 1801

    ≡ Rhizoctonia crocorum (Pers. : Fr.) DC. 1815

    ≡ Thanatophytum crocorum (Pers. : Fr.) Nees 1816

= Rhizoctonia dauci Rabenh. 1855

= Rhizoctonia medicaginis DC. : Fr. 1815

= Tubercularia persicina Ditmar 1817  Note: From Tuberculina persicina - Hyperparasite on rusts on Abies, Senecii; Serjanic and Wedelia (Carmichael et al. Genera of Hyphomycetes. p. 193. 1980; Solheim. Mycotaxon 8(2):394. 1979).

    ≡ Tuberculina persicina (Ditmar) Sacc. 1881

= Rhizoctonia rubiae M.J. Decne. 1837

Notes: Thanatophytum crocorum is the oldest name and has priority, but Helicobasidium purpureum is in much heavier use and likely to be conserved; they are the type species of their respective genera and are synonymous.

Distribution: Cosmopolitan.

Substrate: Roots.

Disease Note: Violet root rot.

Host: Numerous hosts in numerous families.

Supporting Literature:

Lutz, M. , Bauer, R., Begerow, D., and Oberwinkler, F. 2004. Tuberculina - Thanatophytum/Rhizoctonia crocorum - Helicobasidium: a unique mycoparasitic-phytoparasitic life strategy. Mycol. Res. 108: 227-238.
