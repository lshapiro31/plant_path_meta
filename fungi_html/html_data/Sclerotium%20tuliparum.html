Nomenclature data for Sclerotium tuliparum

Rhizoctonia tuliparum Whetzel & J.M. Arthur 1924 (Basidiomycetes, Polyporales)

Notes: Andersen & Stalpers (Mycotaxon 51:451. 1994) considered this fungus to be synonymous with R. solani, but specimens not attributable to R. solani exist and the situtation is still unclear. See: Rhizoctonia Species: Taxonomy, Molecular Biology, Ecology, Pathology and Disease Control 1996, pp 279-288. ------ No sexual state is known and the ultimate disposition of this species has yet to be determined. ------ The name Rhizoctonia tuliparum is ascribed solely to Whetzel & Arthur since Sclerotium tuliparum Kleb. is a later homonym of S. tuliparum Schlechtend.

Distribution: Temperate regions.

Supporting Literature:

Andersen, T.F., and Stalpers, J.A. 1994. A check-list of Rhizoctonia epithets. Mycotaxon 51: 437-457.

Mordue, J.E.M. 1974. Rhizoctonia tuliparum. C.M.I. Descr. Pathog. Fungi Bact. 407: 1-2.

= Sclerotium entogenum Westend. 1852

[= Botrytis parasitica Cavara 1888 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]  Note: Not Pers. : Fr. 1796 nor Schleid. 1845. Nom. illeg.

[= Botrytis tulipae Hopkins 1921 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]  Note: As '(Lib.) Hopkins' comb. nov. Not Lind 1913. Nom. illeg.

[= Sclerotium tulipae Weinm. 1836 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]

= Sclerotium tulipae Lib. 1830

= Sclerotium tuliparum Schltdl. 1831

Notes: Nom. nov. for B. parasitica Cavara. Boerema and Hamers (1988) discussed the nomenclature for this fungus. Conidial synanamorph. From Sclerotium tulipae - Not Lib. 1830. Nom. illeg. From Sclerotium tulipae - Anamorph.

Distribution: Cosmopolitan.

Disease Note: Tulip fire.

Supporting Literature:
