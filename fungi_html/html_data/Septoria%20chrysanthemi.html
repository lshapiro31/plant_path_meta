Nomenclature data for Septoria chrysanthemi

Septoria chrysanthemella Sacc. 1895 (Ascomycetes, Mycosphaerellales)

[= Septoria chrysanthemi Rostr. 1897 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]  Note: From Septoria rostrupii - A nomen novum.

    ≡ Septoria rostrupii Sacc. & P. Syd. 1899

[≡Septoria chrysanthemi Cavara 1892 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]  Note: Not Allesch 1891 [1892?]. Nomen illeg.

= Septoria chrysanthemi-indici Bubák & Kabát 1907

= Septoria varians Joffrin 1901  Note: Valid publication not confirmed.

Notes: Saccardo renamed Sepotoria chrysanthemi Cavara (1892) as Septoria chrysanthemella since it was a later homonym of Septoria chrysanthemi Allesch. (1891). Grove (1935) did not follow the distinction between Allescher's and Cavara's names made by Saccardo and considered the former as accepted. That position was rejected by Punithalingam and Wheeler (1965) based on the uncertainty of whether Grove examined type material of Allescher. Per Verkley et.al. (2013), S. chrysanthemella represents a species complex containing several cryptic species that has yet to be resolved.

Distribution: Cosmopolitan.

Substrate: Living leaves.

Disease Note: Leaf spot, defoliation.

Host: Chrysanthemum spp. (Asteraceae); reports from other Asteraceae and Dipsacaceae.

Supporting Literature:

Grove, W.B. 1935. British Stem- and Leaf-Fungi (Coelomycetes) Vol. 1. Cambridge Univ. Press, Cambridge, 488 pages.

Priest, M.J. 2006. Fungi of Australia. Septoria. ABRS, Canberra; CSIRO Publishing, Melbourne, 259 pages.

Septoria chrysanthemi Halst. 1893 (Ascomycetes, Mycosphaerellales)

Notes: Not Allesch 1891 [1892?] or Cavara 1892. This is a nomen illeg. See Halsted, 1893, Bull. Torrey Bot. Club 20: 251. This forgotten name may be a synonym of something else. Its distribution is uncertain due to the number of homonyms.

Distribution: North America (USA: NJ (type)).

Host: Chrysanthemum leucanthemum (Asteraceae).
