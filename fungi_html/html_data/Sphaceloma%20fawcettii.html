Nomenclature data for Sphaceloma fawcettii

Elsinoe fawcettii Bitanc. & Jenkins 1936 (Ascomycetes, Myriangiales)

    Variant spelling Elsinoe fawcetti Bitanc. & Jenkins 1936  Note: Spelling corrected (Art. 60.11).

= Sphaceloma fawcettii var. fawcettii Jenkins 1925

  ≡Sphaceloma fawcettii Jenkins 1925  Note: See type variety.

  Variant spelling Sphaceloma fawcetti Jenkins 1925

= Sphaceloma fawcettii var. scabiosa (McAlpine & Tryon) Jenk. 1936

  Variant spelling Sphaceloma fawcettii var. scabiosae (McAlpine & Tryon) Jenk.

Notes: Ciferri (1938) created the combination Sphaceloma citri (E. J. Butler) Cif. 1938 based on Sporotrichum citri Butler 1924 arguing that this was the oldest epithet for this fungus. Stalpers (1984) felt that Sporotrichum citri Butler 1925 was probably identical to Sporothrix schenckii Hektoen & Perkins based on the description, but the type could not be located. Tan et al. (1996) and Hyun et al. (2001) support E. fawcettii and S. fawcettii var. scabiosa as different pathotypes of the same species. From Sphaceloma fawcettii var. scabiosa - Tan et al. (1996) and Hyun et al. (2001) support E. fawcettii and S. fawcettii var. scabiosa as different pathotypes of the same species.

Distribution: Worldwide in citrus-growing regions. Asia, Africa, South America (Brazil), Central America, North America (Mexico, USA: HI, FL).

Substrate: Leaves, twigs, fruits.

Disease Note: Sour orange scab, common citrus scab.

Host: Citrus spp. and other Rutaceae. Most varieties of sweet orange and lime are resistant.
