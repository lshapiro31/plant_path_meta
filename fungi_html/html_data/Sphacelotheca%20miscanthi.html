Nomenclature data for Sphacelotheca miscanthi

Sporisorium scitamineum (Syd.) M. Piepenbr., M. Stoll & Oberw. 2002 (Ustilaginomycetes, Ustilaginales)

≡ Ustilago scitaminea Syd. 1924  Note: Nom. cons.

= Ustilago amadelpha Syd., P. Syd. & E.J. Butler 1912  Note: Nom. rej.

= Sphacelotheca miscanthi W.Y. Yen 1937

    ≡ Sporisorium miscanthi (J.M. Yen) L. Guo 1990

= Ustilago scitaminea var. sacchari-barberi Mundk. 1940

= Ustilago scitaminea var. sacchari-officinarum Mundk. 1940

Notes: The taxonomy of Ustilago and related genera is in flux, however, the type of Sporisorium forms a clade with this taxon (Stoll et al. 2005). Some including Vanky (2011) prefer to retain this name in Ustilago because of historical use and the unresolved nature of the problem of delimiting these genera. Ustilago amadelpha Syd., P. Syd. & E.J. Butler is a rejected name (Art. 14.7).

Distribution: Widespread, especially subtropical and tropical regions.

Substrate: Culms.

Disease Note: Sugarcane smut.

Host: Poaceae especially Saccharum spp. Also Imperata, Miscanthus.
