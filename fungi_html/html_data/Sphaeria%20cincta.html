Notes: From Sphaeria cincta - Not Sphaeria cincta Fr.:Fr. 1823. Sphaeria cincta Fr.:Fr. was sanctioned by Fries (Syst. Mycol. II:387) and therefore is treated as if conserved against the earlier homonym Sphaeria cincta DC 1815 (Art. 15.1).

Substrate: Twigs, bark.

Host: Betula spp. (Betulaceae), reports from other hardwoods.

Cytospora cincta Sacc. 1884 (Ascomycetes, Diaporthales)

≡Leucocytospora cincta (Sacc.) Höhn. 1928

= Sphaeria cincta Fr. : Fr. 1823  Note: From Valsa cincta - Adams et al. (2005) treat Leucostoma as a synonym of Valsa.

    ≡ Leucostoma cinctum (Fr. : Fr.) Höhn. 1928

  Variant spelling Leucostoma cincta (Fr. : Fr.) Höhn. 1849

    ≡ Valsa cincta (Fr. : Fr.) Fr. 1849

  Variant spelling Leucostoma cincta (Fr. : Fr.) Höhn. 1849

Notes: From Sphaeria cincta - Not Sphaeria cincta DC.. Sphaeria cincta Fr.:Fr. was sanctioned by Fries (Syst. Mycol. II:387) and therefore is treated as if conserved against the earlier homonym Sphaeria cincta DC 1817 (Art. 15.1).

Distribution: Asia, Australia, Europe, North America (Canada, USA).

Substrate: Dead or dying, attached or fallen twigs.

Disease Note: Necrosis of twigs, perennial canker. Infection occurs through scars, resulting in dieback of branches or whole trees (Hayova & Minter 1998).

Host: Rosaceae, mainly Prunoideae.

Supporting Literature:

Dhanvantari, B.N. 1982. Relative importance of Leucostoma cincta and L. persoonii in perennial canker of peach in southwestern Ontario. Canad. J. Pl. Pathol. 4: 221-225.

Hayova, V.P., and Minter, D.W. 1998. Leucostoma cinctum. I.M.I. Descr. Fungi Bact. 1361: 1-3.
