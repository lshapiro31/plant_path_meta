Nomenclature data for Sphaeria maculiformis

Ramularia endophylla Verkley & U. Braun 2004 (Ascomycetes, Mycosphaerellales)

= Sphaeria maculiformis Pers. 1797

    ≡ Mycosphaerella maculiformis (Pers. : Fr.) J. Schröt. 1894

    ≡ Sphaerella maculiformis (Pers. : Fr.) Auersw. 1869

  Variant spelling Sphaeria maculaeformis Pers. 1797

= Sphaeria punctiformis Pers. 1794

    ≡ Mycosphaerella punctiformis (Pers. : Fr.) Starbäck 1889

    ≡ Sphaerella punctiformis (Pers. : Fr.) Rabenh. 1869

    ≡ Astoma punctiforme (Pers. : Fr.) Gray 1821

    ≡ Diatrype punctiformis (Pers. : Fr.) Zahlbr. 1931

Notes: Use of oldest specific epithet blocked by Ramularia punctiformis Sacc. (1904). Other possibly available epithets based on poorly known taxa, so Ramularia endophylla proposed for conservation in order to provide nomenclatural stability. ---- Mycosphaerella punctiformis is the type for Mycosphaerella. For additional synonyms, see Aptroot (2006).

Distribution: Cosmopolitan.

Substrate: Living and dead leaves.

Disease Note: Leaf spot.

Host: Numerous hardwoods.

Supporting Literature:

Aptroot, A. 2006. Mycosphaerella and its anamorphs: 2. Conspectus of Mycosphaerella. Centraalbureau voor Schimmelcultures, Utrecht, 231 pages.

Verkley, G.J.M., Crous, P.W., Groenewald, J.Z., Braun, U., and Aptroot, A. 2004. Mycosphaerella punctiformis revisited: morphology, phylogeny, and epitypification of the type species of the genus Mycosphaerella (Dothideales, Ascomycota). Mycol. Res. 108: 1271-1282.

Videira, S.I.R., Groenewald, J.Z., Verkley, G.J.M., Braun, U., and Crous, P.W. 2015. The rise of Ramularia from the Mycosphaerella labyrinth. Fung. Biol. 119: 823-843.
