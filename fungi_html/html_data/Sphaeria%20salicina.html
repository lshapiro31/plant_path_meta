Cytospora fugax (Bull. : Fr.) Fr. 1823 (Ascomycetes, Diaporthales)

≡ Variolaria fugax Bull. : Fr. 1791

= Sphaeria salicina Pers. : Fr. 1796

    ≡ Valsa salicina (Pers. : Fr.) Fr. 1849

= Valsa salicina var. tetraspora Fr.

= Valsa socialis Ellis & Everh. 1897

Notes: According to Saccardo (Syllog. Fung. III: 261, 1884), the asexual state of Valsa salicina is Cytospora salicis, but Spielman (1985) did not include Cytospora salicis in her synonymy. The epithet fugax has priority if the two are considered synonymous. From Valsa salicina var. tetraspora - Listed by Hayova 1998, but not listed by Spielman. Listed on IF as a form. Year unknown.

Distribution: Europe, Asia, North America.

Substrate: Bark of twigs.

Host: Principal host: Salix spp.; additional hosts may include trees of various woody Angiosperm plant families.

Supporting Literature:

Hayova, V.P., and Minter, D.W. 1998. Valsa salicina. I.M.I. Descr. Fungi Bact. 1369: 1-3.

Spielman, L.J. 1985. A monograph of Valsa on hardwoods in North America. Canad. J. Bot. 63: 1355-1378.

Wang, Y.-L., Lu, Q., Decock, C., Li, Y.-X., and Zhang, X.-Y. 2015. Cytospora species from Populus and Salix in China with C. davidiana sp. nov. Fung. Biol. 119: 420-432.
