Notes: Consists of two chemotypes producing different mycotoxins (Anderson 2003).

Distribution: Cosmopolitan.

Substrate: Leaves, roots, paper, textiles, soil and plant debris, straw, wood.

Disease Note: Leaf blight, root rot - few reports as a primary pathogen and most often as a secondary invader; produces mycotoxins responsible for stachybotryotoxicosis in animals including humans.

Host: Tillandsia tenuifolia (Bromeliaceae). Glycine max (Fabaceae); numerous other hosts and substrates.
