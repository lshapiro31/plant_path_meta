= Diplodina delphinii Laskaris 1950

    ≡ Ascochyta laskarisii Melnik 1971  Note: Year changed from 1973.

Notes: Depazea aquilegiae Rabenh. (1852) provides the oldest available epithet, which should have been used upon transfer to Calophoma; consequently, Calophoma aquilegiicola is nomenclaturally superfluous and illegitimate (Art. 52.1). From Diplodina delphinii - Dec. 1950 not Laskaris July 1950. From Ascochyta aquilegiae - Not (Roum. & Pat.) Sacc. 1884. From Diplodina delphinii - July 1950 not Golovin Dec. 1950.

Distribution: Africa, Asia, North America; Europe; New Zealand.

Substrate: Leaves and stems.

Disease Note: Dark leaf spots, stem lesions, crown rot.

Host: Aquilegia spp. and other Ranunculaceae.
