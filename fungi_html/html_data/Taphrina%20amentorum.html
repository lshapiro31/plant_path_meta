Nomenclature data for Taphrina amentorum

Taphrina alni (Berk. & Broome) Gjaerum 1966 (Taphrinomycetes, Taphrinales)

≡ Ascomyces alni Berk. & Broome 1876

= Exoascus alni var. strobilinus Thüm. 1880

= Exoascus alnitorquus f. alni-incanae J.G. Kühn 1873

     Taphrina alni-incanae (J.G. Kühn) Sadeb. 1890

    ≡ Taphrina alni-incanae (J.G. Kühn) Magnus 1890

= Exoascus amentorum Sadeb. 1888

  ≡Taphrina amentorum (Sadeb.) Rostr. 1890

= Ascomyces tosqquinetii var. strobilina Rostr. 1880

Notes: The combination "Exoascus alni-incanae" J.G. Kühn was cited in Saccardo but apparently never actually published. See Ray (1939) for more information. Neither was "Taphrina alni-incanae (J.G. Kühn) Sadeb." 1890, a name correctly attributed to Magnus.

Distribution: Asia (Japan), Europe, North America.

Substrate: On catkins.

Disease Note: Catkin deformation ("alder tongues").

Host: Alnus incana, possibly also A. glutinosa (Betulaceae).

Supporting Literature:
