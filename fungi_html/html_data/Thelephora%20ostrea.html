Nomenclature data for Thelephora ostrea

Stereum ostrea (Blume & T. Nees) Fr. 1838 (Basidiomycetes, Russulales)

≡ Thelephora ostrea Blume & T. Nees 1826

= Thelephora fasciata Schwein. 1822

    ≡ Stereum fasciatum (Schwein.) Fr. 1838

= Thelephora lobata Kunze ex Fr. 1830

    ≡ Stereum lobatum (Kunze ex Fr.) Fr. 1838

Notes: Synonomy follows Lentz (1955); see that work for numerous additional synonyms. Thelephora fasciata Schwein. provides the oldest epithet but is unpriorable due to the sanctioning of T. ostrea.

Distribution: North and South America, Africa, Asia, Europe.

Substrate: Hardwoods, rarely on gymnosperms.

Disease Note: Saprobic; white rot.

Host: Numerous hosts.

Supporting Literature:

Lentz, P.L. 1955. Stereum and allied genera of fungi in the Upper Mississippi Valley. Agric. Monogr. U.S.D.A. 24: 1-74.
