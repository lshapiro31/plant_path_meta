Nomenclature data for Uredo dioscoreicola

Uredo dioscoreae-doryphorae J.R. Hern. & E.T. Cline 2010 (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

[≡Uredo spinulosa Y. Ono 1982 - illegitimate later homonym, not included in search]  Note: Not (Cooke) Sacc. 1891 nec Dietel 1897. Nom. illeg.

Notes: This is a nom. nov. Uredo spinulosa Y. Ono 1982 is an illegitimate later homonym of Uredo spinulosa (Cooke) Sacc. 1891, also not Dietel 1897 (= Uredo dieteliana). Ono published Uredo spinulosa Ono 1982 non (Cooke) Sacc. 1891 to validate Uredo dioscoreicola Sawada 1943 nom. inval. (not Uredo dioscoreicola F. Kern & al. 1933). Sawada published a description of this rust in Japanese, but failed to provide a Latin description, therefore the name was not validly published. Uredo dioscoreicola F. Kern & al. 1933 is a different widespread rust on Dioscorea spp. Uredo spinulosa (Cooke) Sacc. 1891 is known only from the type, from an undetermined host sp. in India.

Distribution: Asia (Taiwan, type).

Substrate: Leaves.

Disease Note: Autoecious rust.

Host: Dioscorea doryophora (Dioscoreaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Hernandez, J.R., and Cline, E.T. 2010. Goplana dioscoreae-alatae nom. nov. and other Uredinales on Dioscoreaceae: nomenclature and taxonomy. Mycotaxon 111: 263-268.

Ono, Y. 1982. Rusts of yams in Southeast Asia and South Pacific. Trans. Brit. Mycol. Soc. 79: 423-429.

Updated on Jul 20, 2010

Uredo dioscoreicola F. Kern, Cif. & H.W. Thurston 1933 (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

Notes: Collections from Puerto Rico previously misidentified by Arthur (1924) as Uredo dioscoreae Henn. 1896 were given the new name Uredo dioscoreicola by Kern et al. (1933). Kern (1933) erroneously cited Uredo dioscoreae Arthur 1924 as if this were the basionym, but Arthur (1924) did not publish a new name, he merely cited Uredo dioscoreae Henn. Because Kern et al. (1933) provided a separate description, Uredo dioscoreicola was nevertheless validly published. Stevenson (1975) also mistakenly listed Uredo dioscoreae Arthur 1924 as a synonym of Uredo dioscoreicola Kern et al. 1933, as if Arthur (1924) had published a new name.

Distribution: Asia (Taiwan), South America (Brazil), Caribbean Islands (Dominican Republic, Puerto Rico, Virgin Islands, Cuba).

Substrate: Leaves.

Disease Note: Rust.

Host: Dioscorea polygonoides and Rajania cordata (Dioscoreaceae).

Supporting Literature:

Kern, F.D., Ciferri, R., and Thurston Jr., H.W. 1933. The rust-flora of the Dominican Republic. Ann. Mycol. 31: 1-40.

Stevenson, J.A. 1975. Fungi of Puerto Rico and the American Virgin Islands. Contr. Reed Herb. 23: 743.
