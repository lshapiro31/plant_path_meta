Notes: Simpson et al. (2006) cited by Beenken (2017), in which Caeoma eugeniarum is cited as "the earliest published name for the uredinial state of Puccinia psidii" and provides the oldest epithet which ought to have been adopted upon transfer to Austropuccinia. From Puccinia jambolani - Hennen (1982) lists this as jambolana, and gives a citation from 1920, but includes a note that he did not see the literature. From Puccinia subneurophila - This name was published as a replacement name (nom. nov.) for Puccinia psidii. Uredo psidii is a nom. nov created to prevent conflict with Uredo eugeniarum Henn. (1895). From Puccinia neurophila - Not Puccinia nerviphila Grognot 1863.

Distribution: Australia, South America, Central America and Caribbean, North America (Mexico, USA: HI, FL), Africa (South Africa). Asia (Japan); reports from Taiwan and India may be unreliable.

Substrate: Living leaves, flowers, twigs, fruits.

Disease Note: Autoecious rust; may cause severe defoliation. Urediospores may be disseminated by rain.

Host: Myrtaceae, including Psidium guajava (guava), Eucalyptus, and Pimenta dioica (pimento or allspice).
