Nomenclature data for Uromyces salsolae

Uromyces salsolae Reichardt 1877 (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

≡Caeomurus salsolae (Reichardt) Kuntze 1898

= Puccinia polycnemi Libosch. 1817  Note: Possible synonym. The combination Uromyces polycnemi (Libosch.) Tranzsch. 1907 nom. inval. non McAlpine 1906 was not validly published (Art. 33.1).

Notes: Tranzschel (1907) observed that the Uromyces stage of Puccinia polycnemi Libosch. 1817 was morphologically indistinguishable from Uromyces salsolae. In 1907, Tranzschel wrote (transl. from German): Uromyces polycnemi (Liboschitz) would have priority, but I cannot recommend this name. Therefore, he did not explicitly accept the combination, and it is not validly published, despite the erroneous citation by Gjaerum (1986) and Denchev (1995). The previously published Uromyces polycnemi McAlpine 1906 is a different rust from Australia. Both Gjaerum (1986) and Denchev (1995) listed Uromyces salsolae as a synonym of Puccinia polycnemi (as Uromyces polycnemi (Libosch.) Tranzsch., nom. inval.) (but see Hiratsuka 1992).

Distribution: Asia.

Substrate: Leaves.

Disease Note: Rust.

Host: Salsola spp., Petrosimonia spp., and other Chenopodiaceae. The report on Gossypium hirsutum is doubtful.
