Uromyces trifolii var. trifolii (R. Hedw. ex DC.) Fuckel 1870 (Urediniomycetes, Uredinales)

≡ Puccinia trifolii R. Hedw. ex DC. 1805

≡Nigredo trifolii (R. Hedw. ex DC.) Arthur 1906

≡Uredo trifolii (R. Hedw. ex DC.) DC 1808

≡Uromyces trifolii (R. Hedw. ex DC.) Fuckel 1870  Note: The type variety is the accepted name for this species.

= Uromyces flectens Lagerh. 1909  Note: Considered a synonym of Uromyces nerviphilus (Grog.) Hotson 1925, see Hiratsuka 1992 and Walker 1978 for discussion of nomenclature.

Notes: Not the illegitimate later homonym Uromyces trifolii G. Winter 1884. This name is sometimes erroneously cited as (R. Hedw. ex DC) Lev 1847, Har., or J. Schroeter 1890. Many specimens are misidentified; the current species concept includes only the microcyclic rust on white clover (Trifolium repens). See Walker 1978 (also, Laundon 1975) for discussion of nomenclature.

Distribution: North America, South America, Europe, Asia, New Zealand.

Substrate: Living leaves.

Disease Note: Microcyclic rust.

Host: Trifolium repens (Fabaceae). Frequently misapplied to rusts on other Trifolium spp. (see Walker 1978).
