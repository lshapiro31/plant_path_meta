Nomenclature data for Valsa nivea

Cytospora nivea Sacc. 1881 (Ascomycetes, Diaporthales)

= Sphaeria nivea Hoffm. : Fr. 1787  Note: From Leucostoma niveum - From Cytospora nivea - Fuckel (1870) mentioned Valsa nivea and commented that the anamorph was not Cytospora ceratophora, but did not formally make the combination Cytospora nivea (nom. inval.), therefore Saccardo was the first to validly publish this name.

    ≡ Leucostoma niveum (Hoffm. : Fr.) Höhn. 1928

  ≡Valsa nivea (Hoffm. : Fr.) Fr. 1849

Distribution: Asia, Europe, North America.

Substrate: Branches, stems and twigs.

Disease Note: Canker.

Host: Primarily known from Populus (Salicaceae); also Elaeagnus angustifolia (Elaeagnaceae), Juniperus sp. (Cupressaceae), Rosa sp., Malus sp. (Rosaceae).
