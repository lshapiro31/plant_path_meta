Nomenclature data for Valsa sordida

Cytospora chrysosperma (Pers. : Fr.) Fr. 1818 (Ascomycetes, Diaporthales)

≡ Naemaspora chrysosperma Pers. : Fr. 1796

= Cytospora pulcherrima Dearn. & Hansbrough 1934

= Valsa sordida Nitschke 1870

    ≡ Engizostoma sordidum (Nitschke) Kuntze 1898

Distribution: Africa (Morocco, South Africa), Asia, Australia, New Zealand, Europe, North America (Canada, Mexico), South America (Chile).

Substrate: Bark of twigs.

Disease Note: Bark canker of Populus spp. (poplars), also causes necrosis of Salix spp. (willows). Often associated with the pathogenic fungus Leucostoma niveum.

Host: Mostly Populus spp. and Salix spp. (Salicaceae), but also numerous other hardwoods (Hayova & Minter 1998).
