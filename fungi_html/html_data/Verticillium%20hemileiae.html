Nomenclature data for Verticillium hemileiae

Lecanicillium muscarium (Petch) Zare & W. Gams 2001 (Ascomycetes, Incertae sedis)

≡ Cephalosporium muscarium Petch 1931

= Cephalosporium aphidicola Petch 1931

= Verticillium hemileiae Bouriquet & Bassino 1966

= Cephalosporium lefroyi W.T. Horne 1915  Note: Zare & Gams (2001) list this as a possible synonym.

= Oospora necans Sacc. & Trotter 1906  Note: Zare & Gams (2001) list this as a possible synonym.

= Cephalosporium subclavatum Petch 1942  Note: Zare & Gams (2001) list this as a possible synonym.

= Cephalosporium thripsidum Petch 1931

  Variant spelling Cephalosporium thripidum Petch 1931

Notes: This species was previously reported under the name Verticillium lecanii (=Lecanicillium lecanii). Cephalosporium muscarium is the oldest well-documented name available for this fungus, therefore Zare & Gams epitypified it with a dried culture. The teleomorph is not known.

Distribution: Europe (UK, Italy). Predominantly temperate (Zare & Gams 2001).

Substrate: Various substrates including insects and fungal tissues.

Disease Note: A strain of L. muscarium has been developed as a biological control agent for whitefly under the name Mycotal.

Supporting Literature:

Zare, R., and Gams, W. 2001. A revision of Verticillium section Prostrata. IV. The genera Lecanicillium and Simplicillium gen. nov. Nova Hedwigia 73: 1-50.
